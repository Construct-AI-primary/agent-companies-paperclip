# 01400 - Health

The Health discipline covers occupational health, medical surveillance, health incident management, fitness for work, health risk assessment, and medical emergency response for construction and engineering organizations.

## Overview

This discipline ensures employee health and wellbeing, maintains occupational health standards, implements health surveillance programs, manages health incidents, conducts fitness-for-work assessments, and ensures regulatory compliance with occupational exposure limits (OELs), threshold limit values (TLVs), and health regulations.

## Key Responsibilities

- **Occupational Health Management** — Managing workplace health hazards including chemical, physical, biological, and ergonomic risks
- **Medical Surveillance** — Audiometry, spirometry, biological monitoring, and health screening programs
- **Health Incident Management** — Recording, investigating, and reporting occupational illnesses and health incidents
- **Fitness for Work** — Pre-employment medicals, periodic health assessments, and return-to-work evaluations
- **Health Risk Assessment** — Identifying and evaluating workplace health hazards, OEL/TLV monitoring
- **Medical Emergency Response** — First aid provision, emergency medical services coordination, and emergency response planning
- **Health Promotion** — Wellness programs, health education, and preventive health initiatives
- **Regulatory Compliance** — Compliance with OSHA, COSHH, and local health regulations
