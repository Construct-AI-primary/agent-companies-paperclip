## Domain Overview

The Ethics discipline (01100) governs ethical conduct, compliance, and integrity across all construction and engineering operations. It ensures adherence to legal standards, regulatory requirements, and organizational values.

## Key Regulatory Frameworks

### Anti-Bribery & Anti-Corruption
- **FCPA (Foreign Corrupt Practices Act)** — US law prohibiting bribery of foreign officials
- **UK Bribery Act 2010** — Comprehensive anti-bribery legislation covering commercial and public bribery
- **ISO 37001** — Anti-bribery management systems standard
- **OECD Anti-Bribery Convention** — International anti-corruption framework

### Whistleblower Protection
- **EU Whistleblower Directive (2019/1937)** — EU-wide whistleblower protection standards
- **Dodd-Frank Act** — SEC whistleblower program and protections
- **SOX (Sarbanes-Oxley Act)** — Corporate whistleblower protections

### Code of Conduct
- Industry-specific ethical standards for construction and engineering
- Organizational values and behavioral expectations
- Supplier and partner code alignment

## Core Ethics Processes

### 8-Step Whistleblower Management Process
1. **Intake** — Secure, anonymous reporting channels (web, phone, email, in-person)
2. **Triage** — Initial assessment and categorization of reports
3. **Investigation** — Fact-finding and evidence collection
4. **Adjudication** — Determination of findings and recommendations
5. **Remediation** — Corrective actions and disciplinary measures
6. **Closure** — Case resolution and documentation
7. **Reporting** — Management and board reporting
8. **Follow-up** — Monitoring and continuous improvement

### Gifts & Hospitality Limits
- **Thresholds:** Defined monetary limits for gifts (e.g., $50-$200 depending on jurisdiction)
- **Approval:** Manager approval required above standard thresholds
- **Register:** All gifts and hospitality must be recorded in the Gift Register
- **Prohibited:** Cash gifts, gifts to government officials without clearance, gifts during active procurement

### Conflicts of Interest
- **Disclosure:** Mandatory annual disclosure by all employees
- **Review:** Ethics committee review of disclosed conflicts
- **Mitigation:** Recusal, divestment, or third-party oversight
- **Register:** Central register of declared conflicts
