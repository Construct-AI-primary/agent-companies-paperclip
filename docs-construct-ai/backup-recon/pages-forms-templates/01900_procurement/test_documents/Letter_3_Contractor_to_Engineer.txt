Dear Sir/Madam,

We refer to your letter Ref: UIC/DHBEP/E001 dated November 2, 2023, regarding our claim for
under-measurement of concrete works as initially detailed in our letter Ref: BB/DHBEP/C001.

We appreciate your prompt response. Please find attached the following documentation in
support of our claim:

1.Appendix B: Detailed Volumetric Calculations: These calculations clearly demonstrate
that the as-built volumes for the repaired piled foundations (SoW 2.2.4) significantly exceeded
the BoQ estimates. This was due to actual pile depths being greater than anticipated in the
initial BoQ, a necessity confirmed by the "Geotechnical investigations" (SoW 2.1.1)
undertaken post-tender but pre-construction, and reflected in the approved design
revision DWG-STR-FD-012 Rev B. The BoQ appears not to have been fully updated to
reflect these final design parameters.

2.Appendix C: Deck Profile Analysis: This analysis compares the original design profiles
used for BoQ preparation with the finalized profiles in DWG-STR-BD-105 Rev C. Minor but
cumulative increases in deck thickness and camber, necessary for achieving specified
drainage and structural performance, account for a portion of the disputed volume.

3.Appendix D: Pour Logs & QA/QC Records: These records, maintained as per SoW 3.1
"Quality Control," demonstrate that the concrete placement was executed within specified
tolerances and negate any suggestion of significant over-pour or wastage beyond industry
norms. The concrete was placed to achieve the required lines and levels as per the
approved detailed design.

The issue is not one of unauthorized changes or excessive wastage, but rather that the interim
valuations have not fully captured the true in-situ quantities necessitated by the finalized,
approved designs that evolved from the initial tender information. The "Detailed design drawings
and calculations" (SoW Clause 5) provided by us and subsequently approved by your office form
the basis of the works executed.

The cumulative under-measurement of 185 m³ directly impacts our cash flow and contravenes the
spirit of "Payment will be made based on monthly progress payments" (SoW Clause 6.2)
reflecting work duly completed.

We reiterate our request for an urgent joint site re-measurement focused on the specific
foundation elements and deck sections detailed in our appendices. Alternatively, we propose a
meeting with your quantity surveying team to reconcile these figures based on the provided
documentation and as-built survey data.

We trust this detailed submission clarifies our position and facilitates a swift resolution.
