Dear Sir/Madam,

We acknowledge receipt of your letter Ref: BB/DHBEP/C001 dated October 26, 2023, concerning
alleged under-measurement of concrete works in Interim Payment Certificate No. 5.

We have reviewed your submitted Appendix A. However, payments are made based on quantities
derived from the approved Bill of Quantities (BoQ) and measured in accordance with the
contractually agreed method of measurement, cross-referenced with the latest approved
"Detailed design drawings and calculations" (SoW Clause 5).

While we note your reference to drawing revisions DWG-STR-BD-105 Rev C & DWG-STR-FD-012
Rev B, the quantities certified in IPC No. 5 reflect the net in-situ concrete as per these drawings.
The Scope of Work Clause 2.1.2 "Design and Engineering" places responsibility on the contractor
to develop detailed designs, and Clause 2.2.4 "Bridge Widening" requires installation as per
design.

To further investigate your claim of an additional 185 m³:

1.Please provide detailed calculations demonstrating how this additional volume arises from
the approved drawings, specifically highlighting any sections you believe were omitted or
incorrectly calculated by our quantity surveyor.

2.Clarify if any portion of this claimed volume relates to over-pour, formwork deflection, or
wastage, which are generally not considered for payment under the contract.

3.Provide evidence that any variations leading to increased concrete volumes beyond the
tendered BoQ, if not already covered by approved drawing revisions impacting net
quantities, were instructed as a formal variation under SoW Clause 6.3 "Variations and
Changes."

Upon receipt and review of this detailed information, we can then consider the necessity for a
joint re-measurement, focusing on specific disputed elements.
