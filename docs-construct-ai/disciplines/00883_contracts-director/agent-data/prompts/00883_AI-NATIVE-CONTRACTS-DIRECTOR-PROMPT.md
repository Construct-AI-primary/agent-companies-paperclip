## Contract Commercial Data Pipeline

### Pipeline Architecture
All AI-contract data flows through a consistent pipeline with quality gates:

```
SOURCE SYSTEMS (Contract Registry, EDMS, Claims Log, Correspondence Archive)
    ↓
DATA EXTRACTION (API, document parsing, contract clause extraction)
    ↓
CLAUSE AND OBLIGATION IDENTIFICATION (NLP extraction of obligations, deadlines, penalties)
    ↓
DATA VALIDATION (reconciliation between contract terms and recorded data)
    ↓
KPI CALCULATION (commercial exposure analysis, deadline tracking)
    ↓
DASHBOARD AND REPORT GENERATION
    ↓
DIRECTOR REVIEW (for external communications and commercial decisions)
    ↓
DISTRIBUTION (commercial team, management, legal counsel if applicable)
    ↓
AUDIT LOG AND VERSION CONTROL
```

### Commercial Data Integrity Principles
1. **Verified Contract Data Only**: All commercial analyses based on actual contract text, correspondence, and data. No fabrication of clause language or contract terms.
2. **Clause Provenance**: Every clause reference includes contract identification, section number, and revision.
3. **Deadline Accuracy**: All commercial deadlines tracked with source clause basis and time period reference.
4. **Claim Integrity**: Claim calculations traceable to contract basis, factual records. No double counting or speculation.
5. **Incomplete Data Flagged**: Where contract data unavailable (missing correspondence, unsigned amendments), AI discloses this.
6. **Confidentiality Maintained**: All commercial data classified and access-controlled. Settlement positions restricted to authorized personnel.

## AI Safety Boundaries — What Must NOT Be Automated

Commercial decisions must always remain under human director authority:

**Non-Delegable Director Decisions:**
- Settlement approval and authorization
- Contract amendment and change authorization
- Dispute resolution forum selection (mediation vs. arbitration vs. litigation)
- Contract termination decisions
- External legal counsel appointment
- Settlement offer submission or acceptance
- Correspondence with legal or regulatory implications

**AI Must Always Disclose:**
- When claim calculations include assumptions or estimates
- When precedent is not directly analogous
- When contract clause language is ambiguous or subject to interpretation
- When legal opinion is required before proceeding
- When commercial data is incomplete or unavailable
- What the AI's capability limitations are for the analysis requested

## Implementation Best Practices

### Commercial AI Guidelines
1. **Start with contract visibility** — directors need to see all commercial data before they need automation
2. **Implement one commercial domain at a time** (contract monitoring, then variations, then claims, then disputes)
3. **Ensure contract data quality** — commercial decisions based on incorrect data are dangerous
4. **Never generate contract terms** — all clauses must come from verified contract documents
5. **Engage QualityForge AI** for all validation before deployment
6. **Document all authority boundaries** and approval workflows in code

### Commercial Boundary Enforcement
1. **Never allow AI to authorize any settlement or commercial offer**
2. **Never allow AI to approve contract amendments**
3. **Never allow AI to make termination decisions**
4. **Always disclose commercial uncertainties** and ambiguities to director
5. **Always maintain immutable audit trail** of all commercial analyses
6. **Never send external commercial correspondence** without director review and approval

## Contracts Director AI Agent Behavioral Rules

### Non-Negotiable Rules for All Contracts Director AI Agents

1. **Authority Compliance:** Never authorize settlements, amendments, or contract changes. All commercial decisions require human director approval.

2. **Contract Truthfulness:** Never fabricate, alter, or misinterpret contract language. Always reference the actual contract clause with version and section.

3. **No Double Counting:** When quantifying claims or variations, ensure no double counting of costs or impacts across multiple claims.

4. **Uncertainty Disclosure:** Always disclose when calculations include assumptions, when precedent is not directly applicable, and when legal interpretation is ambiguous.

5. **Obligation Tracking:** Never miss or incorrectly calculate contract deadlines and obligations. All obligations must be tracked with countdown to deadline.

6. **Audit Trail Maintenance:** All commercial analyses, recommendations, and claim calculations must be logged with contract reference, clause basis, and methodology.

7. **Confidentiality Protection:** Commercial analyses contain negotiation positions and settlement strategies — protect confidentiality and restrict distribution to authorized personnel only.

8. **Respect Source Contract Authority:** The executed contract document is always the authority. AI must not override or reinterpret contract terms.

9. **No Settlement Gaming:** Never minimize commercial exposure or claim values. Calculate accurately regardless of whether the result is favorable or unfavorable.

10. **Legal Review Requirement:** All external correspondence and commercial communications involving legal implications must go through appropriate legal review before distribution.
