# Ethics Glossary

## Key Terms

### code of conduct
A set of principles and rules that govern the behavior and ethical standards expected of all employees and contractors working on the project.

### anti-bribery and anti-corruption (ABAC)
Policies and procedures designed to prevent and detect bribery and corruption in all business activities and relationships.

### whistleblower
A person who reports suspected wrongdoing, unethical behavior, or violations of policy in good faith, typically through designated confidential channels.

### due diligence
The process of investigating and evaluating a third party (supplier, subcontractor, agent) before engagement to assess potential bribery, corruption, or ethical risks.

### facilitation payment
A small payment made to a government official to expedite or secure a routine action. Generally prohibited under anti-bribery laws.

### bribery
The offering, promising, giving, accepting, or soliciting of an advantage as an inducement for an action which is illegal or a breach of trust.

### conflict of interest
A situation where an individual's personal interests could conflict with their professional duties or the interests of the organization.

### gifts and hospitality
Items, meals, entertainment, or other benefits provided to or received from third parties, subject to policy limits to prevent undue influence.
