# Logistics Glossary

## Key Terms

### site logistics plan
A comprehensive master document defining how a construction site will physically operate across all project phases, including access routes, gates, laydown areas, crane positions, and traffic management.

### laydown area
A designated zone on a construction site for temporary storage, staging, and handling of materials, equipment, and components delivered to site. Also called a staging area or material yard.

### just-in-time (JIT) delivery
A logistics strategy where materials and equipment are scheduled to arrive on site exactly when needed for installation, minimising storage requirements and site congestion.

### delivery slot
A pre-booked time window during which a supplier or haulier is authorised to deliver materials to a specific site gate, with an assigned crane and laydown zone.

### deconfliction
The process of identifying and resolving conflicts between concurrent logistics activities, such as simultaneous deliveries competing for the same crane or gate.

### crane booking
A scheduled reservation for crane time and capacity to offload deliveries or lift materials on site, coordinated through the logistics team.

### traffic management plan (TMP)
A document defining vehicle and pedestrian circulation routes, signage, barriers, and control measures to ensure safe movement on and around a construction site.

### goods receipt note (GRN)
A formal document confirming the receipt, inspection, and acceptance of delivered materials on site, used for inventory tracking and invoice matching.

### material traceability
The ability to track and document the origin, handling, storage, and installation of materials from supplier through to final placement in the works.

### bill of lading
A legal document issued by a carrier to a shipper acknowledging receipt of goods for transport, detailing the type, quantity, and destination of the cargo. Functions as a receipt, contract of carriage, and document of title.

### certificate of origin
A document certifying the country in which exported goods were manufactured. Required by many customs authorities for tariff determination and trade compliance.

### commercial invoice
A document issued by the seller (exporter) to the buyer (importer) describing the goods, their value, and terms of sale. Used by customs to assess duties and taxes.

### customs declaration
A formal statement submitted to customs authorities declaring the nature, origin, and value of goods being imported or exported, used to calculate duties and ensure regulatory compliance.

### customs broker
A licensed professional or firm authorised to act on behalf of importers and exporters in clearing goods through customs, preparing documentation, and paying duties.

### dwell time
The total time a shipment spends at a port, terminal, or border checkpoint waiting for clearance, handling, or onward transport. High dwell times indicate congestion or processing delays.

### pre-clearance
A customs process where documentation and declarations are reviewed and approved before a shipment arrives, reducing clearance time and dwell at the destination port.

### HS code (Harmonized System code)
An internationally standardised numerical system for classifying traded products. Used by customs authorities worldwide to determine applicable duties, taxes, and regulations.

### letter of credit (L/C)
A financial instrument issued by a bank guaranteeing payment to a seller upon presentation of specified documents, commonly used in international trade to reduce payment risk.

### packing list
A document detailing the contents of a shipment, including item descriptions, quantities, weights, dimensions, and packaging type. Used for customs, carrier, and receiving verification.

### through-bill of lading
A single bill of lading covering the entire journey of a shipment across multiple transport modes (e.g., sea, rail, road), issued by one carrier who assumes responsibility for the full journey.

### MSDS (Material Safety Data Sheet)
A document providing information on the properties, hazards, handling, storage, and emergency procedures for hazardous substances. Required for safe storage and transport of chemicals, fuels, and hazardous materials.

### chain of responsibility (CoR)
A legal concept in road transport where all parties in the supply chain (consignor, packer, loader, manager, driver, consignee) share responsibility for ensuring transport compliance with mass, dimension, speed, and fatigue regulations.

### dunnage
Materials (wood, foam, inflatable bags) used to secure and protect cargo during transport by preventing movement, absorbing shock, and providing cushioning.

### bunded storage
A storage area for hazardous liquids (fuel, chemicals, oil) surrounded by a secondary containment system (bund) designed to contain any spills or leaks, typically with 110% of the largest container's volume.

### incoterms
Internationally recognised trade terms published by the International Chamber of Commerce defining the responsibilities, costs, and risk transfer points between buyers and sellers in international transactions.

### freight forwarder
A company that organises and coordinates the shipment of goods on behalf of shippers, arranging transport, documentation, customs clearance, and warehousing across multiple carriers and modes.

### demurrage
A charge levied by a shipping line or port authority when a container or vessel occupies a berth or terminal beyond the allowed free time. Accumulates daily and can become a significant cost.

### detention
A charge incurred when a shipping container is not returned to the carrier's depot within the allowed free period after unloading, or is collected from the depot too early before loading.

### ETA (Estimated Time of Arrival)
The expected date and time a vehicle, vessel, or shipment will arrive at a specified destination. Subject to change based on delays, weather, and routing.

### ETD (Estimated Time of Departure)
The expected date and time a vehicle, vessel, or shipment will depart from its origin or transit point.

### lift-on/lift-off (LoLo)
A cargo handling method where containers or heavy lifts are loaded and unloaded using cranes (as opposed to roll-on/roll-off, which uses wheeled equipment).

### heavy lift
An oversized or overweight cargo item requiring specialised equipment (mobile cranes, SPMTs, heavy-duty forklifts) for loading, transport, and offloading.

### marshalling
The process of sorting, organising, and staging vehicles, containers, or materials in a designated area before dispatch or delivery to ensure efficient loading and sequencing.

### gatehouse
A controlled access point at a construction site where vehicles, personnel, and materials are checked, logged, and directed to the appropriate zones.

### permit to work
A formal, documented system authorising specific high-risk activities (hot work, confined space entry, crane lifts, road closures) after verifying that all safety precautions are in place.

### route survey
An assessment of a proposed transport route to identify physical constraints (bridge heights, road widths, weight limits, turning radii) and plan safe passage for oversized or heavy loads.

### cold chain
A temperature-controlled supply chain for materials sensitive to heat or freezing (paints, adhesives, vaccines, biological reagents), requiring refrigerated transport and storage.

### reverse logistics
The process of planning, implementing, and controlling the efficient flow of materials, equipment, and waste from the point of use back to the point of origin for return, recycling, repair, or disposal.

### non-conformance (logistics)
A deviation from the planned logistics process, such as wrong item delivered, damaged goods, incorrect quantity, missing documentation, or delivery outside the booked time window.

### material management plan
A document defining how materials will be received, stored, protected, issued, and tracked on site, including storage zone layouts, tagging procedures, and access controls.

## Incoterms Reference (Incoterms 2020)

| Incoterm | Full Name | Seller Responsibility | Buyer Responsibility | Risk Transfer Point |
|----------|-----------|----------------------|---------------------|---------------------|
| EXW | Ex Works | Make goods available at premises | All transport, loading, insurance, import | Buyer's premises or designated collection point |
| FCA | Free Carrier | Deliver goods to named carrier, export cleared | Main transport, insurance, import | When goods are delivered to carrier |
| FAS | Free Alongside Ship | Deliver goods alongside vessel at port, export cleared | Loading on vessel, sea freight, insurance, import | When goods are alongside the vessel |
| FOB | Free on Board | Deliver goods on board vessel, export cleared | Sea freight, insurance, import | When goods are on board the vessel |
| CIF | Cost, Insurance, and Freight | Deliver on board vessel, pay sea freight and insurance, export cleared | Import clearance, destination charges | When goods are on board the vessel |
| CIP | Carriage and Insurance Paid To | Deliver to carrier, pay carriage and insurance to destination, export cleared | Import clearance, destination charges | When goods are delivered to first carrier |
| CPT | Carriage Paid To | Deliver to carrier, pay carriage to destination, export cleared | Insurance, import clearance | When goods are delivered to first carrier |
| DAP | Delivered at Place | Deliver to named place, ready for unloading, export cleared | Unloading, import clearance, duties | When goods are available for unloading at destination |
| DPU | Delivered at Place Unloaded | Deliver and unload at named place, export cleared | Import clearance, duties | When goods are unloaded at destination |
| DDP | Delivered Duty Paid | Deliver to destination, all costs, risks, duties, and taxes paid | Unloading at destination | When goods are available at destination |
