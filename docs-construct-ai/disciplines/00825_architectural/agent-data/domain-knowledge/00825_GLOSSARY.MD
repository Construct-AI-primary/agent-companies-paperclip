# Architectural Glossary

## Key Terms

### façade
The exterior face of a building, especially the front. Encompasses cladding systems, glazing, and architectural treatments that define the building's appearance and weather protection.

### fire compartmentation
Division of a building into fire-resistant compartments to limit fire spread. Achieved through fire-rated walls, floors, doors, and fire stopping of penetrations.

### means of egress
A continuous and unobstructed way out of a building consisting of exit access (path to exit), exit (protected path), and exit discharge (path to public way).

### finish schedule
A document specifying the materials and treatments for all floor, wall, and ceiling finishes throughout a building, organized by room or area.

### accessibility
Design features that enable people of all abilities to access and use a building safely and independently, including wheelchair access, tactile aids, and accessible facilities.

### building envelope
The physical separator between the interior and exterior environments of a building, including walls, roof, windows, doors, and foundations.

### cladding
Non-structural outer covering of a building designed to protect the building from weather and provide aesthetic treatment.

### U-value
A measure of heat transmission through a building element (W/m²K). Lower U-values indicate better thermal insulation performance.

### snagging
The process of identifying and recording minor defects or unfinished work at practical completion for the contractor to remedy (also called punch list).

### as-built drawing
A drawing updated to show the actual constructed configuration of a building, incorporating all changes made during construction.
