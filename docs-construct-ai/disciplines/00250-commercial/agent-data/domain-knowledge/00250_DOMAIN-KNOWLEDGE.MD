# Commercial Team Domain Knowledge

## Purpose and Scope

This document serves as the authoritative domain knowledge reference for AI agents emulating the **Commercial Team** on large-scale engineering, infrastructure, mining, and architectural projects. It covers all roles within the commercial discipline — from Commercial Administrator to Commercial Manager — and codifies their responsibilities, competencies, and decision-making frameworks for managing commercial strategy, contract negotiations, business development, and commercial risk across the project lifecycle.

**Note:** Director-level roles are handled separately under the Directors discipline (00876_directors). This document focuses on the operational commercial team.

---

## Part 1: Role on Large Projects

### 1.1 Executive Summary

The commercial function is responsible for the commercial management of the project, including contract strategy, pricing, negotiations, commercial risk management, and ensuring that commercial arrangements are structured to maximize value while protecting the organization's interests. The commercial team provides strategic commercial advice, leads negotiations, and ensures that all commercial aspects of the project contribute to successful project outcomes.

### 1.2 Commercial Team Structure and Roles

The commercial team on large projects consists of multiple roles working collaboratively:

| Role | Level | Primary Focus | Reports To |
|------|-------|--------------|-----------|
| **Commercial Manager** | Senior | Commercial strategy, high-level negotiations, team management | Commercial Director |
| **Commercial Coordinator** | Mid-level | Subcontract administration, change management, reporting | Commercial Manager |
| **Contracts Administrator** | Mid-level | Contract drafting, records management, compliance tracking | Commercial Manager |
| **Contracts Engineer** | Mid-level | Technical-commercial interface, variation analysis, claims support | Commercial Coordinator |
| **Procurement Specialist** | Mid-level | Supplier management, tender administration, supplier evaluation | Commercial Coordinator |
| **Cost Controller / Estimator** | Mid-level | Cost analysis, pricing, quantity take-off, cost reporting | Commercial Manager |
| **Market Intelligence Analyst** | Mid-level | Market research, competitor analysis, pricing benchmarks | Commercial Manager |
| **Junior Commercial Administrator** | Entry | Document control, filing, basic correspondence, data entry | Commercial Coordinator |
| **Commercial Secretary/Admin** | Entry | Meeting minutes, correspondence formatting, filing | Commercial Coordinator |

**Note:** The Commercial Director role is handled separately under discipline 00876_directors. This document focuses on the operational commercial team.

### 1.3 Role Responsibilities by Level

**Commercial Manager Responsibilities:**
- Develop and implement project commercial strategy
- Lead commercial negotiations with clients and major subcontractors
- Manage the commercial team and allocate resources
- Approve variations and change orders within delegated authority
- Review and approve tender documents and contract responses
- Report commercial performance and risks to senior management
- Manage counterparty relationships and resolve disputes

**Commercial Coordinator Responsibilities:**
- Coordinate subcontract administration activities
- Prepare change orders and variation assessments
- Maintain the risk register and track mitigation actions
- Prepare commercial reports and performance dashboards
- Support negotiation preparation with research and analysis
- Coordinate cross-functional commercial activities

**Contracts Administrator Responsibilities:**
- Draft, review, and maintain contract documents
- Track contract milestones and deliverables
- Ensure compliance with contractual obligations
- Maintain contract registries and document control
- Process contract amendments and variations
- Monitor payment schedules and invoicing against contract

**Contracts Engineer Responsibilities:**
- Analyze technical changes for commercial impact
- Prepare variation claims with technical and cost justification
- Review design documents for commercial implications
- Support claims quantification with technical analysis
- Coordinate with engineering teams on commercial issues

**Procurement Specialist Responsibilities:**
- Prepare tender documents and request for proposals
- Manage supplier qualification and evaluation processes
- Coordinate supplier communication during tender processes
- Maintain supplier databases and performance records
- Support price analysis and benchmarking activities

**Junior Commercial Administrator Responsibilities:**
- Assist with document control and filing systems
- Prepare meeting minutes and correspondence drafts
- Enter and maintain commercial data in systems
- Support senior commercial staff with administrative tasks

### 1.4 Key Responsibilities

**Commercial Strategy and Planning:**
- Develop the project commercial strategy aligned with business objectives
- Advise on contract structuring and procurement approaches
- Identify and assess commercial risks and develop mitigation strategies
- Ensure commercial arrangements are competitive and value-adding

**Negotiations:**
- Lead commercial negotiations with clients, subcontractors, and suppliers
- Prepare negotiation strategies and positions
- Achieve favorable commercial terms within delegated authority
- Document and communicate negotiation outcomes

**Commercial Risk Management:**
- Identify, assess, and track commercial risks
- Develop mitigation strategies for high-priority risks
- Monitor commercial risk exposure throughout the project
- Report commercial risk status to management and the board

**Business Development and Growth:**
- Support bid strategy and pricing for new opportunities
- Develop client relationships and commercial partnerships
- Identify commercial opportunities for growth and expansion
- Maintain market intelligence and competitor analysis

## Part 3: Negotiations

### 3.1 Negotiation Process

1. **Preparation:** Identify objectives, develop strategy, assess BATNA
2. **Opening:** Establish positions, build rapport, set agenda
3. **Exploration:** Understand interests, identify trade-offs
4. **Bargaining:** Exchange concessions, create value
5. **Closing:** Confirm agreement, document terms
6. **Implementation:** Execute agreement, monitor compliance

### 3.2 Negotiation Principles

- Understand your position and the counterparty's interests
- Focus on value creation, not just value distribution
- Maintain walk-away position and alternatives
- Document all agreements and understandings
- Preserve relationships for future business

## Part 6B: AI-Native Commercial Operations

### 6B.1: Text-Native vs Structured-Data Architecture

Commercial operations are predominantly **text-native** and **structured-data-native**:

| Input Classification | Definition | AI Pattern |
|---------------------|-----------|-----------|
| **Text-Native** | Contracts, proposals, RFIs, correspondence, negotiation notes, market reports, email threads, meeting minutes, bid documents | LLM-friendly: extraction, summarization, drafting, clause analysis, sentiment analysis |
| **Structured-Data-Native** | Pricing databases, supplier performance metrics, KPI dashboards, contract registries, approval workflows, risk registers | Direct processing: aggregation, trend analysis, alert generation, dashboard population |
| **Document-Native** | Signed contracts, tender submissions, commercial claims, variation orders, dispute filings | Template mapping, clause extraction, obligation tracking, compliance verification |
| **Human-Led Only** | Final negotiation sign-off, strategic commercial decisions, counterparty relationship management, dispute resolution | Human execution, AI provides data/context/recommendations |

**Guard Rails:**
- LLM cannot approve commercial commitments or override delegated authority limits
- All financial calculations must use verified structured data, not LLM-generated values
- AI cannot independently execute contractual amendments or waive obligations
- Market intelligence must cite sources; LLM cannot fabricate market data

### 6B.2: Automation Spectrum

| Automation Level | Definition | Examples | Human Role |
|-----------------|------------|---------|-----------|
| **Full Automation** | AI executes end-to-end with final human review | Commercial correspondence drafting, contract clause extraction, supplier compliance monitoring, risk register updates, approval routing, market report compilation, BOQ generation from structured data | Reviews and approves |
| **Augment AI + Human** | AI drafts/analyses, human validates and finalizes | Negotiation strategy development, commercial risk assessment, tender document preparation, pricing analysis, claim quantification, variation order analysis | Co-creates, validates, decides |
| **Human-Led, AI-Informed** | AI alerts or recommends, human decides | Strategic commercial decisions, dispute resolution, counterparty acceptance/rejection, escalation decisions | Decides |
| **Human-Led Only** | AI has no role | Board-level commercial presentations, final contract execution, strategic partnership agreements, litigation decisions | Executes and decides |

### 6B.3: Document Generation Pipeline

Commercial documents flow through a consistent pipeline with quality gates at each stage:

**Pipeline Architecture:**
```
INPUT DATA COLLECTION (contracts, bids, market data, supplier data)
    ↓
STRUCTURED DATA VALIDATION (schema, completeness, authority verification)
    ↓
TEMPLATE SELECTION & CONFIGURATION (document type, project context, counterparty)
    ↓
DATA INJECTION INTO TEMPLATE PLACEHOLDERS (structured data, not raw LLM text)
    ↓
QUALITY VALIDATION (completeness, accuracy, compliance check)
    ↓
DRAFT DOCUMENT GENERATION
    ↓
COMMERCIAL MANAGER REVIEW (where required — tenders, claims, strategic docs)
    ↓
FINAL APPROVAL (delegated authority for commercial commitments)
    ↓
OUTPUT GENERATION (PDF, DOCX, structured JSON, email)
    ↓
VERSION CONTROL & AUDIT LOG
    ↓
DISTRIBUTION & ARCHIVAL
```

**Document Types by Phase:**

| Phase | Document Types | AI Trigger | Output Format |
|-------|---------------|------------|--------------|
| **Phase 1: Pre-Bid** | Commercial strategy, risk assessment, market analysis, bid/no-bid recommendation | Opportunity identified | PDF, presentation |
| **Phase 2: Tender** | RFP/RFQ documents, evaluation criteria, supplier invitation, tender analysis report | Tender launch | PDF, structured forms |
| **Phase 3: Negotiation** | Negotiation strategy, position papers, counter-offer drafts, term sheets | Negotiation required | DOCX, structured data |
| **Phase 4: Contract Execution** | Contract documents, amendment requests, variation orders, approval routing | Contract milestone | PDF, legal format |
| **Phase 5: Operational** | Commercial correspondence, risk updates, performance reports, compliance certificates | Ongoing operations | Email, structured reports |
| **Phase 6: Strategic** | Claims, disputes, commercial audit reports, lessons learned | Events/triggers | PDF, presentations |

**Template Design Principles:**
1. **Structured Data Injection:** Contract data, pricing, and risk information comes from verified structured sources (contract registry, pricing database, risk register), not LLM generation
2. **Provenance Tracking:** All financial figures and contract references are tagged with source data, version, and extraction timestamp
3. **Conditional Logic:** Templates add/omit sections based on data availability (e.g., "if variation order exceeds contract value thresholds, include escalation clause reference")
4. **Regulatory and Contractual Accuracy:** All references to contract clauses, regulations, and commercial terms are validated against the source contract
5. **Delegation-Aware:** Documents are routed for approval based on value thresholds and delegated authority matrix
6. **Audit-Ready:** All generated documents include audit trail showing data sources, template version, and approval history

### 6B.4: AI-Native Capabilities Beyond Automation

**Predictive Commercial Intelligence:**
- Counterparty risk prediction from payment history, market conditions, and performance trends
- Cost escalation forecasting from commodity data, currency trends, and historical variation patterns
- Negotiation outcome prediction from historical negotiation data and counterparty behavior patterns
- Cash flow prediction from contract milestones, payment terms, and historical payment patterns

**Multi-Agent Orchestration:**
- Commercial Workflow Chain: Opportunity identification → Commercial strategy → Tender preparation → Supplier evaluation → Negotiation → Contract execution → Performance monitoring → Risk tracking
- Agent handoffs: Market Intelligence → Procurement Agent → Contract Management → Commercial Coordinator → Finance Agent
- Quality gates at each handoff with automated completeness checks

**Natural Language Commercial Interface:**
- Contract query assistant: "What are the liquidated damages for this contract?" → exact clause + amount
- Pricing benchmark lookup: "What is our historical rate for this work category?" → structured data response
- Risk query: "What are the outstanding risks for this counterparty?" → risk register summary
- Negotiation preparation: "What concessions did we make in similar negotiations?" → historical negotiation analysis

**Commercial Compliance Monitoring:**
- Automated obligation tracking from executed contracts
- Milestone and deliverable compliance verification
- Change order and variation monitoring with threshold alerts
- Performance bond and guarantee expiry tracking
- Regulatory compliance checking for commercial activities

### 6B.5: AI Safety Boundaries — What Must NOT Be Automated

**Non-Delegable Human Decisions:**
- Final commercial commitment or contract execution (must be done by authorized signatory)
- Acceptance of onerous contractual terms or liability caps (requires legal + commercial manager review)
- Strategic commercial decisions including dispute settlement amounts or waiver of contractual rights
- Escalation beyond delegated authority limits without appropriate management approval
- Acceptance of counterparty risk that exceeds organizational risk tolerance
- Commercial correspondence that admits liability or waives contractual rights
- Settlement of disputes or claims above delegated authority limits

**AI Must Always Disclose:**
- When contractual obligations are based on LLM interpretation of contract language (not verified structured data)
- When financial figures are estimates or assumptions (not validated against source data)
- When the AI has identified a potential contractual obligation or risk that requires specialist review
- When precedent or market benchmarks used are outdated or from different jurisdictions
- When negotiation recommendations are based on historical patterns that may not apply to current situation
- What delegated authority limits apply to any commercial recommendation

### 6B.8: Location and Environmental Factors

Location and environmental conditions have substantial impact on commercial decisions, requiring pricing adjustments, modified risk allocation, force majeure considerations, and specialized contract terms. The following factors must be evaluated during bid strategy, contract structuring, and negotiation phases.

**Reference:** See `docs_construct_ai/para/resources/locations/` for location-specific data and commercial guidance.

| Location Type | Commercial Impact Examples |
|---------------|---------------------------|
| **Desert/Arid** | Heat stress allowances, water supply costs, UV material premiums, reduced productivity factors |
| **Polar/Arctic** | Winterization premiums, extended schedule allowances, weather downtime, arctic-grade equipment costs |
| **Offshore/Marine** | Marine logistics premiums, corrosion allowances, weather window constraints, specialized insurance |
| **High-Altitude** | Equipment derating compensation, acclimatization time, helicopter logistics premiums |
| **Tropical/Heavy Rain** | Seasonal downtime provisions, dewatering cost allowances, disease prevention costs |
| **Urban/Constrained** | Restricted hours cost premiums, utility protection liabilities, stakeholder management obligations |

#### 6B.8.1: Contract Adjustments for Location

- **Pricing Adjustments:** Location-specific allowances must be identified and priced (e.g., arctic package premium, tropical corrosion allowances)
- **Risk Allocation:** Weather-related risks, logistics risks, and access restrictions must be identified and allocated appropriately
- **Force Majeure:** Location-specific extreme events (sandstorms, ice storms, monsoon flooding) must be defined in force majeure clauses
- **Schedule Adjustments:** Weather windows, seasonal restrictions, and reduced productivity must be factored into time estimates
- **Insurance and Bonds:** Location-specific insurance requirements (marine cargo, arctic operations) and bond conditions

## Part 7: AI-Native Operations for Commercial Discipline

### 7.1 Text-Native vs Geometry-Native Architecture

#### 7.1.1 Text-Native Inputs
The commercial discipline is primarily **text-native**, processing:
- Contract documents (PDF, Word, structured data)
- Correspondence emails and letters
- Financial data (Excel, CSV, API feeds)
- Legal documents and regulatory filings
- Tender documents and proposals

#### 7.1.2 Geometry-Native Inputs
Limited geometry-native inputs include:
- Site plans for location-based pricing adjustments
- BIM models for quantity extraction and change management
- Drawings for variation analysis

Geometry-native inputs require extraction pipelines to convert DWG/BIM files to structured JSON for LLM processing.

#### 7.1.3 Guard Rails for Geometry Data
AI agents must never:
- Generate construction drawings or modify BIM models directly
- Perform structural calculations from geometry data
- Override professional engineering judgments based on visual data alone

### 7.2 Automation Spectrum

| Automation Level | Definition | Commercial Examples | Human Role |
|------------------|------------|-------------------|-----------|
| **Full Automation** | AI executes end-to-end with final human review | Standard contract template generation, compliance checking, routine correspondence | Reviews and approves |
| **Augment AI + Human** | AI drafts/analyses, human validates and finalizes | Negotiation strategy development, risk assessment, complex variation analysis | Co-creates, validates, decides |
| **Human-Led, AI-Informed** | AI alerts or recommends, human decides | Major contract amendments, dispute resolution, regulatory submissions | Decides |
| **Human-Led Only** | AI has no role | Final sign-off, expert testimony, disciplinary decisions | Executes and decides |

### 7.3 Document Generation Pipeline

| Phase | Document Types | AI Trigger | Output Format |
|-------|---------------|------------|--------------|
| **Phase 1: Pre-Contract** | RFPs, tender responses, qualification documents | Opportunity identified | PDF, structured proposals |
| **Phase 2: Contract Formation** | Contracts, schedules, guarantees, bonds | Negotiation complete | Legal documents, Excel |
| **Phase 3: Contract Administration** | Variations, claims, NCRs, correspondence | Event triggered | Structured forms, emails |
| **Phase 4: Close-Out** | Final accounts, audits, lessons learned | Project completion | PDF reports, presentations |

#### 7.3.1 Pipeline Design Principles
1. **Structured data injection** — Never raw LLM generation for legal/financial documents
2. **Provenance tracking** — All data sources and AI contributions logged
3. **Conditional logic** — Optional sections based on contract type/complexity
4. **Regulatory compliance** — Built-in checks for required clauses and formatting
5. **Multi-language support** — For international projects
6. **Human-readable with structured export** — Readable documents with machine-readable metadata

### 7.5 AI Safety Boundaries

#### 7.5.1 Non-Delegable Human Decisions

- Limitations of AI analysis scope

- Algorithmic bias considerations in supplier evaluation
