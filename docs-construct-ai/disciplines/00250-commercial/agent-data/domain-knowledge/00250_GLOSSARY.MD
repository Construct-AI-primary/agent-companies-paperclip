# Commercial Glossary

## Key Terms

### commercial strategy
The overall approach to managing the commercial aspects of a project to maximize value and protect organizational interests.

### negotiation
A structured discussion between parties aimed at reaching a mutually acceptable agreement on commercial terms.

### BATNA (Best Alternative to a Negotiated Agreement)
The best outcome a party can achieve if negotiations fail, providing a walk-away position.

### commercial risk
The potential for financial loss or adverse outcome arising from commercial decisions or market conditions.

### value proposition
The unique value that an organization offers to its clients, expressed through benefits, differentiators, and competitive advantages.

### bid strategy
The approach and positioning used to win a project or contract, including pricing, value proposition, and commercial terms.

### due diligence
The investigation and assessment of a counterparty's capabilities, financial stability, and reputation before entering into a commercial agreement.

### automation spectrum
A classification system that categorates commercial tasks by the level of AI autonomy allowed: Full Automation, Augment AI + Human, Human-Led AI-Informed, and Human-Led Only.

### structured data injection
The process of filling document templates with verified data from databases (contract registry, pricing database, risk register) rather than relying on LLM-generated content.

### provenance tracking
The practice of tagging all data elements in commercial documents with their source, version, and extraction timestamp for audit and verification purposes.

### delegated authority
The approved limits within which a person or system can make commercial commitments on behalf of the organization without requiring further approval.

### commercial obligation tracking
The systematic monitoring of contractual obligations, milestones, deliverables, and compliance requirements from executed contracts.

### clause extraction
The NLP process of identifying and isolating specific contractual clauses (e.g., liquidated damages, termination, force majeure) from contract documents for analysis and reference.

### counterparty risk
The risk that a contract counterparty may fail to fulfill its obligations due to financial, operational, or other factors.

### approval routing
The automated process of directing commercial documents and decisions through the appropriate approval chain based on value thresholds and delegated authority matrix.

### commercial compliance monitoring
The continuous verification that contract obligations, regulatory requirements, and organizational policies are being met throughout the contract lifecycle.

### text-native input
Data sources consisting primarily of unstructured text (contracts, correspondence, reports) that LLMs can process directly.

### structured-data-native input
Data sources from databases, APIs, or structured formats that require direct processing rather than LLM interpretation.

### document generation pipeline
The end-to-end process for producing commercial documents: data collection, validation, template selection, data injection, quality checks, approval, output, and archival.

### multi-agent orchestration
The coordinated workflow where specialized AI agents (Market Intelligence, Procurement, Contract Management, Finance) work sequentially or cooperatively to complete a commercial process.

### predictive commercial intelligence
AI capabilities that forecast commercial outcomes: counterparty risk, cost escalation, negotiation results, cash flow from contract data.
