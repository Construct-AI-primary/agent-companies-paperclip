# Scheduling Glossary

## Key Terms

### critical path
The longest sequence of activities through the project network that determines the minimum project duration. Activities on the critical path have zero or negative total float.

### total float
The amount of time an activity can be delayed from its early start without delaying the project completion date. Calculated as: Late Finish − Early Finish or Late Start − Early Start.

### free float
The amount of time an activity can be delayed without delaying the early start of its successor activity.

### baseline schedule
The approved version of the project schedule used as a basis for measuring performance and managing changes.

### critical path method (CPM)
A scheduling algorithm that calculates early and late start/finish dates for activities and identifies the critical path based on the logical relationships between activities.

### time impact analysis (TIA)
A delay analysis method that models the impact of a delaying event by inserting it into the schedule at the time of occurrence and measuring its effect on the project completion date.

### finish-to-start (FS)
A logical relationship where the successor activity cannot start until the predecessor activity has finished. The most commonly used relationship in construction scheduling.

### look-ahead schedule
A short-term schedule extracted from the master schedule showing planned activities for the next 3 to 6 weeks, used for detailed work planning at the workface level.

### windows analysis
A delay analysis method that divides the project into time periods (windows) and analyzes schedule performance within each window to identify the causes of delay.

### negative float
A condition where the calculated late dates are earlier than early dates, indicating the project is behind schedule and the completion date will be delayed unless recovery action is taken.
