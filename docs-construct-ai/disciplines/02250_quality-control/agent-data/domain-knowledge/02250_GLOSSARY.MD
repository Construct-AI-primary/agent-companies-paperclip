# Quality Control Glossary

## Key Terms

### quality control (QC)
Product-oriented activities that verify quality requirements have been fulfilled through inspection, measurement, and testing of products, materials, and work.

### Inspection and Test Plan (ITP)
A document defining the inspection and test activities, acceptance criteria, and responsibilities for a specific work process.

### hold point (H)
A stage beyond which work cannot proceed without approval from the designated inspection authority.

### witness point (W)
A stage where the inspector is notified; work may proceed if the inspector does not attend.

### Material Test Report (MTR)
A document certifying the material's chemical and mechanical properties, matching the delivered material by heat number.

### non-destructive testing (NDT)
Examination methods that detect flaws without damaging the component. Includes RT, UT, MT, PT, VT.

### radiographic testing (RT)
NDT method using X-rays or gamma rays to detect volumetric defects in welds and materials.

### ultrasonic testing (UT)
NDT method using high-frequency sound waves to detect internal defects and measure thickness.

### magnetic particle testing (MT)
NDT method using magnetic fields and surface particles to detect surface and near-surface flaws in ferromagnetic materials.

### penetrant testing (PT)
NDT method using dye penetrant and capillary action to detect surface defects.

### welding procedure specification (WPS)
A document providing the required welding parameters for a specific joint configuration.

### procedure qualification record (PQR)
A record documenting the test results of a qualified welding procedure.

### dimensional verification
Checking installed components against specified tolerances for position, level, alignment, and orientation.

### quality dossier
A complete collection of quality records (ITPs, MTRs, NDT reports, test certificates, NCRs) for a system or work package, compiled for handover.
