# Quantity Surveying Glossary

## Key Terms

### bill of quantities (BOQ)
An itemized schedule of all quantities and descriptions of work, materials, and labour required to complete a construction project. Prepared using standard measurement rules such as NRM, CESMM, or POMI. Used for tender pricing, valuation, and cost control.

### interim valuation
A periodic (typically monthly) assessment of the value of work completed, materials on site, and other certifiable items, to determine the interim payment due to the contractor.

### extension of time (EOT)
A contractual remedy granted to the contractor when project completion is delayed by events at the employer's risk. Extends the contract completion date without penalizing the contractor for liquidated damages.

### liquidated damages (LDs)
Pre-agreed compensation payable by the contractor to the employer for delays in achieving the completion date. Must be a genuine pre-estimate of loss.

### retention
A percentage (typically 5-10%) deducted from each interim payment and held by the employer to secure rectification of defects. Usually capped at a maximum amount and released partially at practical completion and fully at the end of the defects liability period.

### variation
A change to the contracted scope of work, issued formally through the contract administrator. Includes additions, omissions, substitutions, or modifications to quantities, quality, or work methods.

### cost plan
A detailed breakdown of the estimated project cost by element or trade, used to monitor and control expenditure during the design and construction phases.

### daywork
A method of valuing minor works or unforeseen variations based on actual time, materials, and plant rates rather than measured quantities. Used when the work cannot be priced from BOQ rates.

### final account
The agreed total sum payable to the contractor at project completion, including the original contract sum plus all adjustments for variations, remeasurement, claims, and contract provisions.

### prime cost (PC) sum
An allowance included in the BOQ for work or materials to be selected by the employer or engineer after the contract is awarded. The final value is determined once the specific items are selected.

### provisional sum
An allowance in the BOQ for work or costs not sufficiently defined at tender stage. The amount is expended only if instructed by the contract administrator.

### re-measurement
A contract type or mechanism where quantities are remeasured based on actual work executed, with payment based on actual quantities × BOQ rates.

### defects liability period (DLP)
A period (typically 12 months from practical completion) during which the contractor is responsible for rectifying defects that arise. Retention is held during this period.

### advance payment
An early payment made to the contractor for mobilization, recovered through deductions from subsequent interim payments at a defined rate per payment.

### daywork rate
An agreed hourly or daily rate for labour, plant, or materials used to value minor variations or work that cannot be quantified.
