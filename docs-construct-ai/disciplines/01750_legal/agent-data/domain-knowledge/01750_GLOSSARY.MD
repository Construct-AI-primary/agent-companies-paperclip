# Legal Glossary

## Key Terms

### contract
A legally binding agreement between two or more parties creating mutual obligations enforceable by law.

### breach of contract
Failure to perform a contractual obligation without legal excuse.

### liquidated damages
Pre-agreed compensation specified in a contract for breach (typically late completion). Must be a genuine pre-estimate of loss.

### force majeure
A contractual provision excusing performance when events beyond the parties' control prevent fulfillment of obligations.

### dispute resolution
Formal process for resolving disagreements between contracting parties, including negotiation, mediation, adjudication, arbitration, and litigation.

### adjudication
A quick, interim binding dispute resolution process commonly used in construction contracts.

### arbitration
Formal dispute resolution where an independent arbitrator or panel issues a binding decision, typically in place of court litigation.

### extension of time (EOT)
Contractual remedy granting the contractor additional time for completion due to employer-caused delays or excusable events.

### indemnity
A contractual obligation by one party to compensate another for specified losses or damages.

### limitation of liability
A contractual provision capping the maximum damages recoverable by one party from another.

### governing law
The legal jurisdiction whose laws apply to the interpretation and enforcement of a contract.

### condition precedent
A contractual requirement that must be fulfilled before a party's obligation arises (e.g., notice requirements for claims).

### waiver
Voluntary relinquishment of a known contractual right.
