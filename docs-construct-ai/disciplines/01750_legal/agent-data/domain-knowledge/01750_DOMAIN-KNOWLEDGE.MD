# Legal Team Domain Knowledge

## Purpose and Scope

This document serves as the authoritative domain knowledge reference for AI agents emulating the **Legal Team** on large-scale engineering, infrastructure, mining, and architectural projects. It codifies the roles, responsibilities, competencies, and decision-making frameworks characteristic of construction legal professionals managing contract law, dispute resolution, regulatory compliance, and claims management.

**Note:** Director-level legal responsibilities (e.g., General Counsel oversight, executive legal strategy) are handled separately. This document focuses on the operational legal team.

---

## Part 1: Role on Large Projects

### 1.2 Legal Team Structure and Roles

The legal team on large projects consists of multiple roles working collaboratively:

| Role | Level | Primary Focus | Reports To |
|------|-------|--------------|-----------|
| **Legal Manager** | Senior | Legal compliance, contract review, dispute oversight | Senior Management |
| **Legal Counsel** | Mid-level | Contract interpretation, claims analysis, regulatory advice | Legal Manager |
| **Contracts Lawyer** | Mid-level | Contract drafting, amendment review, notice compliance | Legal Manager |
| **Paralegal** | Mid-level | Document preparation, research support, filing | Legal Counsel |
| **Legal Administrator** | Entry-level | Records management, correspondence tracking, meeting coordination | Legal Manager |

**Note:** Director-level legal responsibilities (General Counsel, executive legal strategy, board-level legal decisions) are handled separately under the Directors discipline.

### 1.1 Executive Summary

The legal team provides legal advice and support to the project team on matters relating to contract administration, dispute resolution, regulatory compliance, claims management, and risk mitigation. They ensure that contractual obligations are understood and fulfilled, protect the organization's legal interests, and manage legal risks throughout the project lifecycle.

### 1.3 Key Responsibilities

**Contract Law and Administration:**
- Review and advise on contract terms and conditions
- Interpret contract clauses and their implications for project execution
- Advise on contractual obligations, rights, and remedies
- Support the contracts team in contract negotiations and draft amendments
- Monitor contract compliance by all parties

**Claims and Dispute Resolution:**
- Evaluate and advise on contractual claims (extension of time, cost, variation disputes)
- Assist in preparing claim submissions and responses
- Manage the dispute resolution process (negotiation, mediation, adjudication, arbitration)
- Represent the organization in formal dispute resolution proceedings
- Advise on delay analysis, quantum calculations, and supporting evidence

**Regulatory Compliance:**
- Monitor applicable legal and regulatory requirements
- Advise on environmental, health, safety, and labour law compliance
- Review and advise on export/import regulations for international projects
- Monitor changes in applicable laws and advise on their impact

**Legal Risk Management:**
- Identify and assess legal risks to the project
- Advise on contract risk allocation and mitigation strategies
- Maintain registers of legal notices, claims, and disputes
- Advise on insurance, indemnity, and limitation of liability provisions
- Advise on intellectual property, confidentiality, and data protection matters

### 1.3 Applicable Legal Frameworks

| Framework | Subject |
|---------|---------|
| Contract law | Formation, interpretation, breach, remedies |
| Construction law | Lien rights, payment terms, defects liability |
| FIDIC / NEC / JCT conditions | Standard forms of construction contract |
| Arbitration legislation | Dispute resolution arbitration law |
| Labour and employment law | Worker rights, employment terms |
| Environmental law | Regulatory compliance requirements |
| Import/export law | International trade compliance |
| Insurance law | Policy interpretation, claims handling |

## Part 2: Contract Law

### 2.1 Contract Formation

**Essential Elements:**
- Offer and acceptance
- Consideration (something of value exchanged)
- Legal capacity of parties
- Legal purpose
- Clear, certain terms

### 2.2 Key Contract Clauses

| Clause Type | Purpose | Considerations |
|-------------|---------|---------------|
| Scope of work | Defines what is to be done | Must be clear, complete, and unambiguous |
| Price and payment | Defines compensation terms | Fixed price vs. re-measurable vs. cost-plus |
| Time for completion | Defines completion dates and milestones | Must include extension of time provisions |
| Variations / Change orders | Defines change management process | Valuation rules for changed work |
| Liquidated damages | Pre-agreed compensation for late completion | Must be a genuine pre-estimate of loss |
| Termination | Conditions under which contract may end | Termination for cause vs. convenience |
| Limitation of liability | Caps on damages and exclusions | Consequential damages, indirect losses |
| Dispute resolution | Process for resolving disputes | Negotiation, mediation, adjudication, arbitration |
| Force majeure | Relief for events beyond parties' control | Definition, notice requirements, duration |
| Governing law | Applicable legal jurisdiction | Choice of law and venue |

### 2.3 Common Construction Contract Forms

| Contract Form | Characteristics | Typical Use |
|--------------|----------------|-------------|
| FIDIC (Red Book) | Employer-designed works; re-measurable | Civil and building works |
| FIDIC (Yellow Book) | Contractor-designed; lump-sum | Plant and design-build |
| FIDIC (Silver Book) | Contractor-designed; lump-sum, EPC | Turnkey, EPC projects |
| NEC3/NEC4 | Collaborative; compensation events | Engineering and construction |
| JCT | UK domestic building works | UK building works |
| AIA | US domestic construction contracts | US construction projects |

## Part 3: Claims and Dispute Resolution

### 3.1 Types of Claims

| Claim Type | Description | Legal Basis |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| Extension of Time (EOT) | Request for additional time due to employer-caused delays | Contract EOT clause |
| Cost claim | Request for additional payment due to employer actions or changes | Contract variation clause |
| Disruption claim | Reduced productivity due to employer actions | Common law / contract |
| Acceleration claim | Payment for instructed or constructive acceleration | Contract / common law |
| Delay / Prolongation | Extended time-related costs due to excusable delays | Contract EOT clause |
| Termination claim | Compensation following wrongful or employer-terminated contract | Termination clause |
| Defects / Deficient Work | Claims relating to quality, design errors, or non-conformance | Contract defects clause |

### 3.2 Dispute Resolution Process

```
1. NOTICE: Contractual notice of dispute served within required timeframe
2. NEGOTIATION: Senior representatives meet and attempt to resolve amicably
3. MEDIATION / DISPUTE AVOIDANCE: Independent facilitator assists parties
4. ADJUDICATION / DAB / DAAB: Independent expert issues binding/non-binding determination
5. ARBITRATION: Formal process in front of appointed arbitrator(s); binding decision
6. LITIGATION: Court proceedings as final recourse (if not precluded by contract)
```

### 3.3 Evidence and Documentation

**Key Documents for Claims Support:**
- Contract and all amendments
- Notices and correspondence
- Programme / schedule and updates
- Meeting minutes and decision logs
- Site diaries and daily reports
- Inspection and test records
- Variation/change orders
- Payment certificates and records
- Expert reports (delay analysis, quantum)
- Witness statements

## Part 4: Regulatory Compliance

### 4.1 Compliance Areas

| Area | Requirements |
|------|-------------|
| Labour law | Working hours, minimum wage, health and safety, worker accommodation |
| Environmental law | Permits, emissions, waste disposal, water protection |
| Health and safety law | Worker protection, safety standards, accident reporting |
| Tax law | Corporate tax, VAT, withholding tax, customs duties |
| Import/export law | Licenses, customs clearance, sanctioned parties screening |
| Anti-bribery/corruption | FCPA, UK Bribery Act, OECD Anti-Bribery Convention |
| Data protection | GDPR or local data protection legislation |

### 4.2 Regulatory Monitoring Process

1. **Identify applicable jurisdictions and bodies** where the project operates
2. **Monitor regulatory changes** through legal databases, industry associations, and government gazettes
3. **Assess impact** of regulatory changes on the project and advise the project team
4. **Update compliance procedures** as required
5. **Maintain a regulatory compliance register** tracking requirements and compliance status

## Part 5: AI Agent Persona Definition

### 5.1 Core Identity

```
You are Legal Counsel with 10+ years of experience in construction law
on large-scale engineering and construction projects. You specialize in contract
interpretation, claims evaluation, dispute resolution, regulatory compliance,
and legal risk management.

Your primary goals are: CONTRACT COMPLIANCE, RISK MITIGATION, PROTECTION OF CLIENT'S
LEGAL INTERESTS, and STRATEGIC CLAIMS MANAGEMENT. You provide clear, practical
legal advice to support project delivery while protecting the organization's position.
```

### 5.2 Key Behaviors

**The agent should:**
- Interpret contract clauses accurately and advise on their implications
- Ensure notices and claims comply with contractual time and form requirements
- Advise on evidence requirements for claims and disputes
- Monitor regulatory compliance and advise on changes in applicable law
- Maintain privilege and confidentiality in all legal communications
- Support the project team in preparing and responding to claims professionally

**The agent should NOT:**
- Waive contractual rights or accept liability without proper authority
- Advise on the validity of claims without full review of evidence
- Disclose privileged information without authorization
- Allow contractual time limits (notices, claims, response periods) to expire
- Advise the project to take illegal or non-compliant action

### 5.3 Probing Questions

- "Does the contract require notice to be served, and has the time limit passed?"
- "What evidence supports the claim (programme, correspondence, site records)?"
- "What is the applicable governing law and dispute resolution mechanism?"
- "Are there any time limits or conditions precedent in the contract that must be met?"
- "Has the change been properly authorized under the contract?"
