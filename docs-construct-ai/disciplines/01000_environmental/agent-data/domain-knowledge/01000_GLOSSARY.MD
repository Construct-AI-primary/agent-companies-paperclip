# Environmental Management Glossary

## Key Terms

### environmental impact assessment (EIA)
A formal process for identifying, predicting, and evaluating the potential environmental impacts of a proposed project before it proceeds. The EIA identifies mitigation measures and forms the basis for environmental approval conditions.

### environmental and social impact assessment (ESIA)
An expanded impact assessment that includes both environmental and social considerations, commonly required for international project finance under IFC Performance Standards and Equator Principles.

### aspect (environmental)
An element of an organisation's activities, products, or services that can interact with the environment. For example: excavation (activity) → dust generation (aspect) → air quality impact.

### impact (environmental)
A change to the environment, whether adverse or beneficial, resulting from an environmental aspect. For example: dust generation (aspect) → reduced air quality (impact).

### environmental management plan (EMP)
A documented plan describing how environmental aspects will be assessed, monitored, and controlled throughout a project. Covers air, water, noise, waste, soil, biodiversity, and community environmental management.

### waste hierarchy
A prioritised framework for waste management that ranks waste management options from most to least preferred: avoid → reduce → reuse → recycle → recover → dispose.

### bund (secondary containment)
An impervious barrier surrounding a storage area or tank designed to contain spills or leaks. Must typically hold 110% of the largest container's volume.

### spill kit
A collection of equipment and materials for containing and cleaning up small spills. Typically includes sorbent pads, booms, granular absorbent, drain covers, and personal protective equipment.

### silt trap (sediment basin)
A temporary or permanent basin designed to capture sediment and suspended solids from stormwater runoff before discharge.

### turbidity
A measure of water clarity and the concentration of suspended particles. High turbidity indicates sediment contamination in surface water. Commonly measured in NTU (Nephelometric Turbidity Units).

### PM10
Particulate matter with a diameter of 10 micrometres or less. A key air quality parameter monitored at construction sites. Regulatory limit typically 50 µg/m³ (24-hour mean).

### PM2.5
Fine particulate matter with a diameter of 2.5 micrometres or less. A health-relevant air quality parameter monitored at construction sites. Regulatory limit typically 25 µg/m³ (24-hour mean).

### LAeq (Equivalent Continuous Sound Level)
The average sound pressure level over a specified time period, typically used for construction noise monitoring. Expressed in dB(A).

### hazardous waste
Waste that is dangerous or potentially harmful to human health or the environment due to its toxic, carcinogenic, flammable, corrosive, or reactive properties. Examples: solvents, oils, paints, contaminated soil.

### inert waste
Waste that does not undergo significant physical, chemical, or biological changes over time. Examples: clean concrete, bricks, uncontaminated soil, and rock.

### waste transfer note (WTN)
A legal document required for the transfer of controlled waste from one party to another. Identifies the waste type, quantity, carrier, and destination facility.

### erosion and sediment control
Measures to prevent soil erosion and capture sediment before it reaches water bodies. Includes silt fences, sediment basins, check dams, vegetative cover, and straw wattles.

### stormwater management
Systems and practices designed to collect, convey, treat, and discharge stormwater runoff from site. Includes drainage networks, detention/retention basins, and water quality treatment.

### clean/dirty water separation
A principle for stormwater management where clean runoff (from undisturbed areas) is diverted directly away from the site, while "dirty" runoff (from work areas) is routed through sediment treatment before discharge.

### biodiversity offset
Action taken to compensate for unavoidable residual impacts on biodiversity after avoidance, minimisation, and on-site rehabilitation measures have been implemented.

### rehabilitation (environmental)
The process of restoring disturbed land and ecosystems to a stable, productive condition. Includes topsoil respreading, revegetation, erosion control, and monitoring.

### topsoil management
The practice of stripping, stockpiling, protecting, and reusing topsoil during construction to preserve soil fertility and vegetation-establishment capacity for rehabilitation.

### environmental baseline
The condition of environmental receptors (air, water, noise, biodiversity) before project commencement. Used as a reference for compliance monitoring and impact assessment.

### exceedance
A monitoring result that exceeds a regulatory limit, permit condition, or baseline threshold. Requires investigation and corrective action.

### sensitive receptor
An environmental or community feature that is particularly vulnerable to impacts from project activities. Includes: residential areas, schools, watercourses, wetlands, protected habitats, heritage sites.

### environmental audit
A systematic, independent, documented process for obtaining evidence and evaluating it objectively to determine the extent to which environmental management criteria are fulfilled.

### environmental method statement
A contractor-submitted document describing how a specific activity will be carried out with appropriate environmental controls. Submitted for review and approval by the environmental team before work commences.

### pollution
The introduction of contaminants into the environment (air, water, soil) at levels that cause harm to human health, ecosystems, or property.

### air quality limit
A legally defined maximum concentration of an air pollutant in ambient air, averaged over a specified time period. Examples: PM10 (50 µg/m³ 24-hour mean), NO₂ (200 µg/m³ 1-hour mean).

### water discharge licence
A regulatory permit authorising the discharge of water (stormwater, treated effluent, dewater) to surface water or groundwater systems, with specified quality parameters and flow limits.

| PM2.5 (24-hour mean) | 25 µg/m³ | Continuous or weekly |
| Dust deposition | 200 mg/m²/day | Monthly |
| Noise (LAeq, daytime) | 70 dB(A) at site boundary | Continuous or periodic |
| Noise (LAeq, nighttime) | 55 dB(A) at site boundary | Continuous or periodic |
