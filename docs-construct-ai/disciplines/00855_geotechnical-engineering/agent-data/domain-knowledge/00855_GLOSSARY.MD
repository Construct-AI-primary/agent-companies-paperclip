# Geotechnical Engineering Glossary

## Key Terms

### bearing capacity
The maximum pressure that soil or rock can safely support without excessive settlement or shear failure. Ultimate bearing capacity is divided by a factor of safety to give allowable bearing capacity.

### settlement
Vertical downward movement of a structure or soil layer due to loading. Includes immediate (elastic) settlement and consolidation settlement (time-dependent in clay).

### Standard Penetration Test (SPT)
An in-situ test where a split-barrel sampler is driven into soil by repeated hammer blows. The N-value (blows per 300mm) is used to estimate soil properties.

### Cone Penetration Test (CPT)
An in-situ test where a cone is pushed into the ground at constant rate, measuring tip resistance, sleeve friction, and pore pressure. Used for continuous soil profiling.

### Atterberg limits
Tests that characterize the moisture-dependent behavior of fine-grained soils: liquid limit, plastic limit, and shrinkage limit.

### consolidation
Time-dependent volume reduction of soil due to expulsion of water from pores under sustained loading. Primary concern for structures on clay.

### compaction
Densification of soil by mechanical means to improve bearing capacity and reduce settlement. Measured as percentage of standard or modified Proctor density.

### slope stability
The ability of a soil or rock slope to resist failure along a sliding surface. Analyzed using limit equilibrium or finite element methods with a factor of safety.

### differential settlement
Uneven settlement between different parts of a structure. More critical than uniform settlement as it induces structural stress.

### liquefaction
Loss of strength in saturated, loose granular soils during seismic shaking, causing the soil to behave like a liquid.

### dewatering
The removal of groundwater from a construction site to permit dry working below the water table.

### retaining wall
A structure designed to hold back soil and resist lateral earth pressure. Types include cantilever, gravity, anchored, and reinforced earth walls.
