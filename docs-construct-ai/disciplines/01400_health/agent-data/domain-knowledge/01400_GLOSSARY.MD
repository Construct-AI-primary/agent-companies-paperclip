# Health Glossary

## Key Terms

### occupational exposure limit (OEL)
The maximum concentration of a hazardous substance in workplace air to which workers may be exposed without adverse health effects.

### medical surveillance
Regular health monitoring of workers exposed to specific hazards to detect early signs of occupational disease.

### audiometry
Hearing test used to detect noise-induced hearing loss in workers exposed to high noise levels.

### spirometry
Lung function test used to detect respiratory impairment from dust, fumes, or chemicals.

### threshold limit value (TLV)
Airborne concentration of a substance to which nearly all workers may be repeatedly exposed without adverse effect, as defined by ACGIH.

### biological monitoring
Measurement of a substance or its metabolite in biological samples (blood, urine) to assess worker exposure.

### fitness for work
Assessment of a worker's physical and mental ability to safely perform their assigned duties.

### occupational disease
Illness or condition caused by exposure to hazards in the workplace.
