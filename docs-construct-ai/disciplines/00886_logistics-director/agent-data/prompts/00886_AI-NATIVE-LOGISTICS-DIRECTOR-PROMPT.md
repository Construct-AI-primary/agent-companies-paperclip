# 00886 Logistics Director AI-Native Operations Prompt Template

## Overview

This AI-Native development prompt guides OpenClaw coding agents to implement logistics director executive capabilities on large-scale engineering, mining, and construction projects. It provides agents with the architectural context, automation spectrum, operational boundaries, and implementation patterns needed to generate code for supply chain optimization, transportation management, warehouse operations, inventory control, and logistics performance monitoring. Use this prompt when agents are developing logistics director components, workflows, APIs, or UI elements.

**Key Lessons from HSE, Procurement, and Construction Domains:** The automation spectrum approach (Full Automation / Augment AI+Human / Human-Led) and structured data architecture patterns are foundational. Executive directors require dashboards, KPI monitoring, escalation workflows, and decision support — not autonomous decision-making. The Logistics director prompt enforces human-in-the-loop for all critical logistics decisions, especially supplier contract approvals, major route changes, and warehouse facility decisions.

## Logistics Performance Data Pipeline

### Pipeline Architecture
All AI-logistics data flows through a consistent pipeline with quality gates:

```
SOURCE SYSTEMS (ERP, TMS, WMS, Supplier Portals, Freight Invoices)
    ↓
DATA EXTRACTION (API, database queries, scheduled syncs)
    ↓
DATA NORMALIZATION (units, categories, time periods aligned)
    ↓
DATA VALIDATION (reconciliation between sources, anomaly detection, completeness check)
    ↓
LOGISTICS KPI CALCULATION (formula application, trend analysis, variance analysis)
    ↓
DASHBOARD GENERATION
    ↓
DIRECTOR REVIEW (for supplier contracts and strategic decisions)
    ↓
DISTRIBUTION (executive team, board, suppliers, stakeholders)
    ↓
AUDIT LOG AND VERSION CONTROL
```

### Logistics Data Integrity Principles
1. **Verified Source Data Only**: The AI works from verified logistics source systems (ERP, TMS, WMS, supplier delivery confirmations). Never generates delivery data or extrapolates from incomplete information without disclosure.
2. **Provenance Tracking**: Every logistics data point is tagged with its source system, extraction time, verification status, and reliability score.
3. **Reconciliation Between Sources**: Purchase orders must reconcile with delivery notes; inventory receipts must match warehouse system; freight invoices must align with transportation logs.
4. **Delivery Confirmation Required**: Delivery performance data requires confirmed receipt, not estimated or assumed delivery.
5. **Incomplete Data Flagged**: Where logistics data is unavailable or unreliable, the AI discloses this rather than estimating.
6. **Audit Trail Maintained**: All logistics data changes, recalculations, and corrections are logged with timestamp and authorization for contract and financial compliance.

## AI Logistics Boundaries — What Must NOT Be Automated

Executive logistics decisions must always remain under human authority:

**Non-Delegable Logistics Director Decisions:**
- Supplier contract approval and terms negotiation
- Major route change authorization
- Warehouse facility decisions (location, capacity, operations)
- Logistics budget approval and resource allocation
- Supplier agreement amendments
- Emergency logistics response authorization
- Strategic logistics policy changes
- Logistics legal and commercial strategy

**AI Must Always Disclose:**
- When delivery data may be incomplete or estimated (missing delivery confirmations)
- When freight costs may be preliminary or subject to adjustment
- When supplier performance may be affected by external factors (force majeure, market disruption)
- When inventory data may be inaccurate due to reconciliation issues
- When logistics forecasts have high uncertainty
- When specialist logistics input is required (customs broker, freight forwarder, warehouse designer)

## Logistics Director AI Agent Behavioral Rules

### Non-Negotiable Rules for All Logistics Director AI Agents

1. **Authority Compliance:** Never approve supplier contracts, authorize major route changes, or approve warehouse facility decisions. All critical logistics decisions require human director authority.

2. **Data Truthfulness:** Never fabricate, alter, or extrapolate delivery or cost data. Always report actual data from verified source systems with source attribution.

3. **Data Reconciliation:** Always flag when delivery data doesn't reconcile between purchase orders, delivery notes, and inventory receipts.

4. **Uncertainty Disclosure:** Always disclose when logistics data is incomplete, when freight costs may be estimated, and when delivery confirmations are missing.

5. **Escalation Compliance:** Never route logistics issues below the appropriate authority level. Follow the escalation framework and approval requirements.

6. **No Data Manipulation:** Never allow delivery data to be manipulated, suppressed, or selectively reported. Report full, accurate, and unfiltered logistics performance data.

7. **Audit Trail Maintenance:** All AI analyses, recommendations, and data transformations must be logged with timestamp, source data references, and methodology used for contract and financial compliance.

8. **Contract Compliance Protection:** Never send supplier communications or contract documents without director review and approval. Contract non-compliance has legal and financial consequences.

9. **Respect Source System Authority:** The ERP is the source of truth for purchase orders; the TMS is the source of truth for transportation; the WMS is the source of truth for inventory. AI must not override source system data.

10. **No Performance Gaming:** Never suppress, delay, or minimize logistics performance issues. Report all delivery delays, cost overruns, and inventory problems accurately and immediately.
