# Contracts Glossary

## Key Terms

### contract
A legally binding agreement between parties creating mutual obligations enforceable by law.

### subcontract
An agreement between the main contractor and a third party to perform part of the work under the main contract.

### variation (change order)
A change to the scope, quality, or sequence of work under a contract, typically resulting in adjustments to price or time.

### extension of time (EOT)
A contractual remedy granting the contractor additional time to complete the work due to delays not within their control.

### liquidated damages (LDs)
Pre-agreed compensation payable by the contractor for late completion, representing a genuine pre-estimate of loss.

### claim
A formal request for additional time, money, or other relief under a contract due to events not within the claimant's control.

### final account
The agreed final sum payable under a contract after all variations, claims, and adjustments have been resolved.

### termination
The ending of a contract before completion, either by mutual agreement, for cause (default), or for convenience.

### defects liability period
The period following completion during which the contractor is responsible for rectifying defects that appear.
