# Quality Assurance Glossary

## Key Terms

### quality assurance (QA)
Process-oriented activities that provide confidence that quality requirements will be fulfilled. QA focuses on preventing defects through proper systems, procedures, and audits.

### quality control (QC)
Product-oriented activities that verify that quality requirements have been fulfilled. QC focuses on identifying defects through inspection and testing.

### Inspection and Test Plan (ITP)
A quality planning document that defines the inspection and test activities, acceptance criteria, and responsibilities for a specific work process or activity.

### hold point (H)
A designated stage in construction or manufacturing activity beyond which work must not proceed without the approval of the designated authority (client, engineer, or third-party inspector).

### witness point (W)
A designated stage where the contractor notifies the designated authority. Work may proceed if the authority does not attend at the agreed time.

### Project Quality Plan (PQP)
A document describing the quality management system, procedures, and resources to be applied to a specific project to meet quality requirements.

### non-conformance
Non-fulfilment of a specified requirement. A product, process, or service that does not meet the specified standard or requirement.

### corrective action
Action to eliminate the cause of a detected non-conformance or other undesirable situation and prevent recurrence.

### audit findings
Results of the evaluation of collected audit evidence against audit criteria. Can be conformances, non-conformances, or observations.

### calibration
Comparison of a measurement device or standard against a traceable standard to determine accuracy and adjust if necessary.
