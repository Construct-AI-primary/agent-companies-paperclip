# Finance Glossary

## Key Terms

### accounts payable (AP)
Money owed by the project to suppliers and subcontractors for goods and services received but not yet paid.

### accounts receivable (AR)
Money owed to the project by clients or customers for goods delivered or work performed but not yet paid.

### accrual
An accounting entry recording a cost or revenue in the period it is incurred or earned, regardless of when payment is made or received.

### cash flow
The movement of money into and out of the project. Positive cash flow means more money coming in than going out.

### committed cost
The value of orders placed (POs, subcontracts) that represent financial obligations but have not yet been invoiced or paid.

### cost code
A unique identifier assigned to each cost element for tracking and reporting purposes, aligned with the project WBS and CBS.

### days sales outstanding (DSO)
The average number of days it takes to collect payment from clients after invoicing.

### general ledger (GL)
The complete record of all financial transactions of the project, organized by account.

### IFRS
International Financial Reporting Standards — globally recognized accounting standards.

### retention
A percentage of payment withheld from a contractor or supplier until defects liability period ends or certain conditions are met.

### three-way match
Verification process matching a purchase order, goods receipt (GRN), and supplier invoice before processing payment.

### withholding tax
Tax deducted at source from payments made to subcontractors, service providers, or non-resident suppliers.
