# Chemical Engineering Glossary

## Key Terms

### process flow diagram (PFD)
A simplified schematic diagram showing the major equipment, process streams, flow rates, compositions, and operating conditions in a chemical process. Does not show instrumentation, valves, or piping details.

### piping and instrumentation diagram (P&ID)
A detailed diagram showing all process equipment, piping, instrumentation, control systems, and safety devices for a plant system. The primary reference for process design, construction, and operation.

### HAZOP (Hazard and Operability Study)
A structured, systematic technique for examining a process design to identify potential deviations from intended operation, their causes, and consequences. Uses guide words (No, More, Less, Reverse) combined with process parameters (Flow, Pressure, Temperature, Level).

### LMTD (Log Mean Temperature Difference)
A mean temperature difference used in heat exchanger design that accounts for the changing temperature difference along the length of the heat exchanger. Formula: LMTD = (ΔT1 − ΔT2) / ln(ΔT1/ΔT2).

### reflux ratio
The ratio of liquid returned to the column (reflux) to liquid withdrawn as product. A key variable in distillation design affecting the number of plates and column diameter.

### LOPA (Layer of Protection Analysis)
A semi-quantitative risk assessment method that evaluates the adequacy of independent protection layers against process hazards. Uses frequency of initiating events and probability of failure of each protection layer.

### PSV (Pressure Safety Valve)
A spring-loaded pressure relief device designed to open rapidly when inlet pressure exceeds a predetermined set pressure and to close when pressure drops below set pressure plus blowdown. Used primarily for gas or vapour service.

### residence time
The average time a fluid element spends inside a reaction vessel or process unit. Critical for conversion and product quality in continuous processes.

### material balance
A fundamental principle stating that mass entering a system minus mass leaving plus mass generated minus mass consumed equals mass accumulated. Used to quantify all material flows in a process.

### energy balance
A fundamental principle stating that energy entering a system minus energy leaving plus heat added minus work done equals the change in internal energy. Used to quantify heat duties, temperatures, and energy flows in a process.
