# Mechanical Engineering Glossary

## Key Terms

### P&ID (Piping and Instrumentation Diagram)
A schematic diagram showing the piping system, equipment, instrumentation, and control logic for a process system. Used as the primary reference for design, construction, and operations.

### isometric drawing (ISO)
A 3D representation of a piping spool or system on a 2D drawing, showing all dimensions, welds, fittings, and component locations. Used for fabrication and installation.

### NPSH (Net Positive Suction Head)
The absolute pressure at a pump suction minus the vapour pressure of the pumped fluid. NPSH available must exceed NPSH required to prevent cavitation.

### MAWP (Maximum Allowable Working Pressure)
The maximum pressure at which a pressure vessel or piping system is permitted to operate at its design temperature, as determined by code calculations.

### hydrostatic test
A pressure test using water (or other liquid) to verify the structural integrity and leak-tightness of pressure equipment. Typically conducted at 1.5 × design pressure.

### NDE/NDT (Non-Destructive Examination/Testing)
Examination methods used to detect flaws in materials and welds without damaging the component. Common methods: RT, UT, PT, MT, VT.

### FAT (Factory Acceptance Test)
A test conducted at the manufacturer's facility to verify that equipment meets the specified requirements before shipment to site.

### SAT (Site Acceptance Test)
A test conducted on-site after installation to verify equipment performance and function in its final operating environment.

### pre-commissioning
Activities performed after installation but before introduction of process fluids: cleaning, flushing, pressure testing, lubrication, and solo runs.

### commissioning
The process of introducing energy and fluids to systems and verifying that they operate as designed, including integration testing and performance guarantee testing.

### pipe stress analysis
Engineering analysis to ensure that piping systems can withstand thermal expansion, deadweight, pressure, and external loads without exceeding allowable stresses or imposing excessive loads on connected equipment.

### pressure vessel rating
The classification of a pressure vessel based on its design pressure, temperature, and material. Governed by ASME Section VIII, EN 13445, or equivalent.

### corrosion allowance
Additional material thickness added to equipment and piping to account for material loss due to corrosion over the design life.

### flange rating
The pressure-temperature class of a pipe flange, per ASME B16.5. Common ratings: 150, 300, 600, 900, 1500, 2500 lb.

### cavitation
The formation and collapse of vapour bubbles in a liquid when local pressure falls below the vapour pressure. Causes damage to pump impellers, valves, and piping.

### thermal expansion
The dimensional change of piping and equipment due to temperature variation. Must be accommodated through expansion loops, expansion joints, or flexible routing.

### pipe schedule
A standardized wall thickness designation for pipe, independent of material. Common schedules: SCH 40 (standard weight), SCH 80 (extra strong), SCH 160 (double extra strong).
