# Mixed-Use Development Project Procurement Typology

## Sector Overview

Mixed-use developments combine residential, commercial, retail, hospitality, and sometimes institutional uses within a single building or precinct. These projects are characterized by complex interface management between different use types, phased delivery strategies, diverse procurement streams, and integrated infrastructure. Procurement must balance the requirements of residential finishing, commercial fit-out, retail branding, and common area amenities within coordinated delivery programs.

### Sub-Sectors

| Sub-Sector | Description | Examples |
|-----------|-------------|----------|
| **Mixed-Use Buildings** | Vertical mixed-use with multiple functions in one structure | Residential towers with retail podium, office with ground-floor retail |
| **Precincts** | Horizontal mixed-use across multiple buildings on a site | Master-planned communities, urban regeneration precincts |
| **Waterfront Developments** | Mixed-use on waterfront sites with marine infrastructure | Marina precincts, waterfront promenades, canal districts |
| **Transit-Oriented Developments** | Mixed-use integrated with public transport hubs | Station precincts, bus terminal developments, park-and-ride |

## Key Goods vs Services Distinctions for Mixed-Use

### Goods (PO Items)
- Diverse procurement streams across residential, commercial, retail, and hospitality
- Facade and cladding with thermal and acoustic separation between uses
- MEP equipment with zone-specific controls and tenant metering
- Lifts with destination control for mixed-use traffic patterns
- Common area finishes with high durability and aesthetic requirements
- Marine infrastructure materials with corrosion resistance (waterfront)

### Services (WO/Service Order Items)
- Complex interface management between different use types
- Phased delivery and handover across residential, retail, and commercial zones
- Tenant fit-out coordination with brand and design guideline compliance
- MEP commissioning with zone-by-zone testing and BMS integration
- Common area finishing with quality and durability standards

## Project-Specific Procurement Considerations

- **Mixed-Use Buildings:** Vertical logistics coordination, tower crane utilization, MEP riser zoning, separate metering per use type
- **Precinct Developments:** Phased delivery across multiple buildings, shared infrastructure (roads, utilities), common area management
- **Waterfront Developments:** Marine works coordination, tidal construction windows, corrosion-resistant materials, public access requirements
- **Transit-Oriented Developments:** Transport authority coordination, pedestrian flow management, security integration, noise mitigation
- **Phased Occupancy:** Early handover of retail/commercial while residential continues, tenant coordination, common area completion sequencing
- **Integrated Infrastructure:** Shared parking, district cooling/heating, centralized BMS, waste management systems, security integration
