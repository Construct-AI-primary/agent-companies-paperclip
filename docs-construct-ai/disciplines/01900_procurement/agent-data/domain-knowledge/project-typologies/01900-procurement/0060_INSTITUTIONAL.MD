# Institutional Facilities Project Procurement Typology

## Sector Overview

Institutional projects encompass hospitals and healthcare facilities, schools, universities, government buildings, and public service facilities. Procurement involves specialized institutional equipment, complex MEP systems, durable building finishes, and compliance with stringent regulatory standards. Institutional procurement is characterized by strict building codes, accessibility requirements, specialized functional spaces, and long-term durability considerations.

### Sub-Sectors

| Sub-Sector | Description | Examples |
|-----------|-------------|----------|
| **Healthcare** | Hospitals, clinics, medical centers, specialized treatment facilities | General hospitals, specialist hospitals, day clinics, emergency centers |
| **Education** | Schools, universities, research facilities, libraries | Primary/secondary schools, university campuses, lecture theaters, laboratories |
| **Government** | Administrative buildings, civic centers, courthouses | Municipal offices, parliament buildings, courthouses, police stations |
| **Public Service** | Community facilities, cultural institutions, sports venues | Museums, libraries, community centers, sports stadiums, fire stations |

## Key Goods vs Services Distinctions for Institutional

### Goods (PO Items)
- Medical equipment with regulatory certification (CE/FDA), radiation shielding, and room integration requirements
- Medical gas systems with purity certification and HTM/NFPA compliance
- Laboratory equipment with ISO certification and specialized ventilation requirements
- Lifts with hospital/stretcher specifications and emergency operation modes
- MEP equipment with healthcare ventilation standards and BMS integration
- Flooring with infection control, anti-static, and slip resistance requirements
- Sanitaryware with clinical grade, anti-ligature, and accessibility standards

### Services (WO/Service Order Items)
- Medical equipment installation with radiation shielding certification and OEM procedures
- Sterile area construction with ISO 14644 clean room certification and infection control
- Laboratory fit-out with ISO 15190 certification and fume cupboard testing
- MEP installation requiring medical gas certification and healthcare ventilation sign-off
- Commissioning with room pressure testing, HEPA certification, and clinical area handover
- Infection control during construction in operational healthcare environments

## Project-Specific Procurement Considerations

- **Hospitals:** Phased delivery to maintain operations, infection control during construction, medical gas certification, imaging equipment lead times, room readiness protocols
- **Clinics/Day Hospitals:** Compact MEP systems, modular medical equipment, outpatient-focused design, accessibility compliance
- **Schools:** Phased construction around academic calendar, durable finishes, IT infrastructure, sports facilities, hall/auditorium equipment
- **Universities:** Laboratory intensive, lecture theatre AV systems, campus-wide BMS, research equipment, library fit-out
- **Government Buildings:** Security integration, public counter fit-out, accessibility compliance, heritage considerations for renovations
- **Museums/Cultural:** Environmental control (humidity/temperature), lighting standards, security systems, exhibit integration
- **Sports Venues:** Seating systems, broadcast infrastructure, crowd management systems, lighting standards, acoustic design
