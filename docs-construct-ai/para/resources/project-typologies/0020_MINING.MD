# Mining & Minerals Project Typology

## Sector Overview

Mining projects involve the extraction and processing of mineral resources (metalliferous minerals, coal, industrial minerals, diamonds, gold, copper, iron ore, etc.). These projects are characterized by large-scale earthworks, remote locations, harsh operating conditions, significant environmental and community impact, and highly regulated permitting processes.

### Sub-Sectors

| Sub-Sector | Description | Examples |
|-----------|-------------|----------|
| **Open-Pit Mining** | Surface extraction with large-scale excavation | Open-pit copper, gold, iron ore, coal mines |
| **Underground Mining** | Sub-surface extraction via tunnels and shafts | Deep-level gold, platinum, coal mines |
| **Mineral Processing** | Concentration, refining, and beneficiation | Concentrators, crushing circuits, flotation plants, smelters |
| **Tailings Management** | Storage of mining waste | Tailings storage facilities (TSFs), tailings dams, paste fill |
| **Artisanal and Small-Scale Mining (ASM)** | Small operations, often informal | Alluvial gold, gemstone, diamond mining |
| **Heap Leaching** | Chemical extraction from low-grade ore | Gold/copper heap leach pads, solvent extraction-electrowinning (SX-EW) |
| **In-Situ Leaching** | Mineral dissolution in the ground without excavation | Uranium, copper in-situ recovery |

## Regulatory and Compliance Regime

### Key Standards and Codes

| Category | Standards |
|----------|-----------|
| Mine Safety | MSHA (US), MHSA (South Africa), local mining regulations |
| Tailings Management | GISTM (Global Industry Standard on Tailings Management), ICMM guidelines |
| Environmental Management | ISO 14001, IFC Performance Standards, Equator Principles |
| Geotechnical | ICOLD guidelines, USACE, SME Engineering guidelines |
| Explosives | Local explosives regulations, UN Recommendations on Transport of Dangerous Goods |
| Water Management | IFC Wastewater Guidelines, local water use legislation |

### Permitting Requirements

- Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA) / Environmental and Social Impact Assessment (ESIA)
- Mining license / mineral rights
- Water use license (abstraction, discharge)
- Air quality and emissions permit
- Explosives storage and handling license
- Tailings facility design approval and ongoing monitoring
- Closure plan and financial provision (rehabilitation trust fund)
- Heritage and cultural impact assessments
- Community development and social license to operate

## Typical Project Delivery Methods

| Method | Typical Use | Pros | Cons |
|--------|-------------|------|------|
| **EPCM (Engineering, Procurement, Construction Management)** | Greenfield mining projects, processing plants | Owner control over major decisions | Owner carries cost and schedule risk |
| **EPC (Engineering, Procurement, Construction)** | Processing plant, well-defined scope | Single point of responsibility | Less flexibility for scope changes |
| **Owner's Team + Multiple Trade Contracts** | Brownfield expansions, remote locations | Owner flexibility, local contractor involvement | Complex coordination and interface management |
| **Mining Contractor** | Ongoing mining operations | Transfer operational risk | Long-term dependency, cost escalation |

## Typical Project Lifecycle

```
1. EXPLORATION
   └── Reconnaissance, geochemical sampling, drilling, JORC/NI 43-101 reporting

2. PRE-FEASIBILITY STUDY (PFS)
   ├── Resource and reserve estimation
   ├── Preliminary mine design and processing flowsheet
   ├── Capital and operating cost estimates (Class 4)
   └── Initial environmental and social screening

3. FEASIBILITY STUDY (FS) / BANKABLE FEASIBILITY STUDY (BFS)
   ├── Detailed engineering (30–60% design maturity)
   ├── Processing test work and metallurgical flowsheets
   ├── Risk analysis and sensitivity modeling
   ├── Class 3 cost estimate (+/- 15%)
   └── Investment decision

4. SANCTION AND PERMITTING
   ├── Final Investment Decision (FID)
   ├── Construction and mining permits
   ├── EPC/EPCM contractor appointments
   └── Long-lead equipment ordering

5. CONSTRUCTION AND COMMISSIONING
   ├── Site access roads and camp establishment
   ├── Earthworks, TSF embankment construction
   ├── Processing plant erection, conveyor installation
   ├── Power supply and water management systems
   └── Pre-commissioning, commissioning, ramp-up

6. OPERATIONS (5–40+ years)
   ├── Drilling, blasting, excavation
   ├── Hauling, crushing, grinding, concentration
   ├── Tailings management and water recycling
   └── Ongoing life-of-mine planning and extensions

7. MINE CLOSURE AND REHABILITATION
   ├── Decommissioning and equipment removal
   ├── Landform reconstruction, topsoil replacement, revegetation
   └── Long-term monitoring (TSF, water quality)
```

## Risk Profile

### Primary Risk Categories

| Risk Category | Examples | Typical Mitigation |
|---------------|---------|-------------------|
| **Geotechnical / Slope Stability** | Pit wall failure, TSF breach, rock falls | Geotechnical monitoring, instrumentation, drainage management |
| **Process / Metallurgical** | Recovery lower than test work predictions | Conservative design, pilot testing, test work contingency |
| **Water Management** | Tailings dam overtopping, acid mine drainage | Robust spillways, water balance management, treatment systems |
| **Community / Social** | Protests, land disputes, illegal mining | Community engagement, local employment, grievance management |
| **HSE** | Explosives misfires, vehicle collisions, dust exposure | Safety management systems, training, PPE, blast protocols |
| **Regulatory** | Permit delays, regulatory changes | Early engagement, compliance tracking, political risk management |
| **Supply Chain** | Reagent availability, equipment spares, fuel supply | Strategic inventory, dual sourcing, on-site generation |
| **Market / Price** | Commodity price volatility affecting viability | Hedging, long-term offtake agreements, flexible production |
| **Environmental** | Acid rock drainage, mercury/cyanide discharge | Containment, monitoring, treatment facilities |

### Insurance and Bonding

- Construction All Risk (CAR) with mining endorsement
- Professional indemnity for engineers and consultants
- Environmental impairment liability
- Political risk insurance (developing countries)
- Financial surety / bonds for closure and rehabilitation
- Business interruption / Delay in Start-Up

## Commercial and Contractual Models

| Aspect | Common Practice |
|--------|----------------|
| Owner | Mining company (junior, major, state-owned) |
| Contract Forms | EPCM (most common for processing plants), lump-sum or reimbursable for construction |
| Mining Operations | Mining contractor (owner-operator or outsourced to mining services company) |
| Payment Milestones | Progress payments tied to engineering/construction milestones, often in USD |
| Retention | 5–10%, released after commissioning or defects liability period |
| Offtake | Long-term off-take agreements with smelters, refineries, or traders |
| Royalties | Government royalties on production or revenue |
| Local Content | Host-country employment and procurement requirements |

## Environmental and Social Considerations

- **Tailings Management:** Global standards (GISTM) emphasize design safety, monitoring, and consequences of failure. Consequences classification (low, significant, high, very high) drives design criteria.
- **Acid Rock/Mine Drainage:** Sulfide-bearing waste rock and tailings can produce acidic discharge; requires containment and potentially water treatment.
- **Water Consumption:** Mineral processing consumes large volumes of water; water recycling and management are critical, particularly in arid regions.
- **Biodiversity Impact:** Mine sites may affect critical habitats; offset and mitigation measures required.
- **Community Resettlement:** Land acquisition and population displacement may be required; must follow IFC Performance Standard 5 or equivalent.
- **Air Quality and Dust:** Blasting, haul roads, and processing generate dust and particulates; requires management plans (water spraying, vegetation).
- **Closure and Rehabilitation:** Mine closure planning begins at project inception; financial provision for rehabilitation is mandatory in most jurisdictions.

## Technology and Innovation Trends

- **Autonomous Mining:** Self-driving haul trucks, autonomous drilling, and remote-controlled excavators
- **Electrification of Mine Fleet:** Battery-electric vehicles (BEVs) for underground and surface mining, reducing diesel consumption
- **Digital Twin and Asset Intelligence:** Real-time monitoring of equipment, processing plants, and TSFs
- **Paste and Filtered Tailings:** Water-efficient tailings management reducing TSF risk
- **In-Situ Recovery:** Non-invasive mineral extraction reducing surface disturbance
- **Renewable Energy Integration:** Solar, wind, and battery storage for off-grid mine power
- **Predictive Maintenance:** AI and IoT for equipment health monitoring and failure prediction
- **Blockchain Traceability:** Supply chain transparency for conflict minerals and ESG compliance
- **Heap Leach Optimization:** Sensor-based monitoring and control for copper and gold heap leaching
