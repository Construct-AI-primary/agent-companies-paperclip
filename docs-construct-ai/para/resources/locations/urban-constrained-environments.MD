# Urban & Constrained Environments

## Environmental Conditions
- **Limited site access**: Narrow streets, restricted vehicle sizes, no dedicated laydown areas
- **Noise restrictions**: Strict noise limits, restricted working hours, vibration limits
- **Neighbor interfaces**: Adjacent buildings, public spaces, traffic disruption concerns
- **Existing utilities**: Congested underground services, live services requiring protection or diversion
- **Public interaction**: Pedestrian flows, public safety, visual impact requirements

## Discipline-Specific Impacts
- **Civil/Structural**: Deep excavations adjacent to existing buildings, underpinning, micro-piling, ground movement monitoring, vibration limits
- **Mechanical**: Compact equipment requirements, crane restrictions (no large mobile cranes), noise attenuation on plant
- **Electrical**: Utility diversions, temporary power from constrained grid connections, underground cable routing
- **HSE**: Public protection, pedestrian diversions, dust/noise management, traffic management, working at height near public areas

## Procurement Considerations
- **PO**: Compact/silent equipment, noise barriers, pre-fabricated modular components (minimize site work), pollution-controlled vehicles, low-emission plant
- **WO**: Utility protection services, traffic management, stakeholder engagement, vibration monitoring, off-site prefabrication, specialist lifting operations
- **SO**: Verify just-in-time delivery schedules, off-peak delivery confirmations, restricted hours compliance, noise specifications

## Health & Safety
- Public exclusion zones and protective canopies/scaffolding
- Certified traffic controllers for road closures and diversions
- Continuous dust and noise monitoring with automatic work stoppage triggers
- Strict working hour compliance to meet local authority conditions

## Logistics & Mobilization
- Just-in-time delivery scheduling with off-peak and night windows
- Off-site pre-fabrication and modular assembly to minimize on-site activities
- Night/weekend deliveries subject to permits and community notification
- Shared logistics yards with adjacent projects

## Mitigation Measures
- Pre-fabrication and modular construction to minimize disruptive site work
- Ground Penetrating Radar (GPR) and trial trenches before any excavation
- Noise and vibration monitoring with automatic work stoppage systems
- Community engagement programs and advance notification protocols
- Comprehensive logistics management plans with delivery scheduling
