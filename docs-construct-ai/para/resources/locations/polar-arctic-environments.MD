# Polar & Arctic Environments

## Environmental Conditions
- **Sub-zero temperatures**: Consistently -20°C to -50°C, extreme lows below -60°C
- **Permafrost**: Permanently frozen ground that thaws with heat disturbance
- **Limited daylight**: Polar night (24-hour darkness) in winter, midnight sun in summer
- **Ice and snow**: Ice loading, snow drifts, ice roads

## Discipline-Specific Impacts
- **Civil/Structural**: Permafrost foundation design, heated concrete enclosures, steel brittleness at low temperatures
- **Mechanical**: Fuel gelling, hydraulic fluid thickening, cold-start failures, ice on components
- **Electrical**: Battery capacity reduction, cable insulation brittleness, condensation on warm-up
- **HSE**: Frostbite, hypothermia, reduced visibility, ice slip hazards

## Procurement Considerations
- **PO**: Arctic-grade materials, low-temperature steel, winterized fuel, heated enclosures, cold-weather PPE
- **WO**: Ice road construction, permafrost monitoring, winterization services, snow removal
- **SO**: Verify low-temperature certifications, winter fuel specs, arctic-grade material confirmations

## Health & Safety
- Cold stress monitoring, buddy system, heated warming shelters
- Frostbite and hypothermia prevention training
- Ice awareness, reduced visibility protocols

## Logistics & Mobilization
- Ice road logistics (seasonal windows), air transport for critical items
- Heated storage and laydown areas
- Pre-positioned materials before freeze-up

## Mitigation Measures
- Heated concrete enclosures with temperature monitoring
- Thermosyphon foundations to maintain permafrost stability
- Arctic-grade lubricants and fuels with anti-gel additives
- Pre-fabricated modular construction to minimize cold-weather site work
- Redundant equipment for cold-start failures
