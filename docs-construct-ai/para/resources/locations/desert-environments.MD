# Desert Environments

## Environmental Conditions
- **Extreme temperatures**: Daytime 45°C+ (113°F+), diurnal swings of 20-30°C
- **Sand and dust storms**: Frequent haboobs with abrasive particulate matter
- **Water scarcity**: Limited natural sources, saline groundwater
- **UV radiation**: Intense solar radiation causing material degradation

## Discipline-Specific Impacts
- **Civil/Structural**: Concrete curing requires moisture retention, accelerated hydration, temperature-controlled mixes
- **Mechanical**: Equipment cooling systems stressed, air filters clogged, lubricant degradation
- **Electrical**: Dust ingress to switchgear, inverter efficiency reduction, cable insulation degradation
- **HSE**: Heat stress, dehydration, sunburn, dust inhalation

## Procurement Considerations
- **PO**: Desert-specification equipment (desert packages), UV-resistant materials, water procurement services
- **WO**: Dust suppression services, AC maintenance, hydration programs, heat stress monitoring
- **SO**: Verify heat-resistant specifications, UV ratings, dust-seal ratings on confirmations

## Health & Safety
- Mandatory heat stress monitoring, work-rest cycles, shaded rest areas
- Respiratory protection during dust storms
- UV protection (clothing, sunscreen), unlimited drinking water

## Logistics & Mobilization
- Sand-swept access roads, dust-controlled laydown areas
- Just-in-time for perishables, bulk water transport
- Sand-filtered storage enclosures for sensitive equipment

## Mitigation Measures
- Night/early morning working schedules
- Concrete curing compounds and chilled mixing water
- Pressurized, filtered cabins for equipment and control rooms
- Desalination or water recycling for construction use
