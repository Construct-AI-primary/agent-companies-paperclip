# High Altitude & Mountainous Environments

## Environmental Conditions
- **Reduced oxygen**: ~3% less per 1000 ft elevation gain; at 3000m, approximately 30% less oxygen
- **Low atmospheric pressure**: Affects combustion, explosive performance, and boiling point of water
- **Steep terrain**: Limited flat areas, challenging access routes, gravity hazards
- **Seismic activity**: Mountain belts often seismically active
- **UV radiation**: Increases approximately 10% per 1000m elevation

## Discipline-Specific Impacts
- **Civil/Structural**: Seismic design requirements, slope stability, rockfall protection, foundations on steep slopes
- **Mechanical**: Engine derating (3% power loss per 1000ft), turbocharging requirements, reduced compressor efficiency
- **Electrical**: Corona discharge at high voltage, reduced cooling efficiency for transformers, lightning protection
- **HSE**: Altitude sickness (AMS, HAPE, HACE), UV overexposure, falls from steep terrain, hypothermia

## Procurement Considerations
- **PO**: High-altitude derated equipment, turbocharged engines, seismic-rated materials, altitude-rated explosives, oxygen supply equipment
- **WO**: Geotechnical stability works, rock bolting services, cable crane operations, medical evacuation services, acclimatization programs
- **SO**: Verify altitude-rated specifications, derating factors, seismic certifications, high-altitude packaging

## Health & Safety
- Altitude acclimatization programs with progressive ascent protocols
- Medical screening for altitude work, supplemental oxygen availability
- UV protection (higher SPF), fall arrest systems, helicopter medevac capability on-site
- Worker rotation schedules to limit exposure

## Logistics & Mobilization
- Cable crane or winch systems for material delivery up steep grades
- Narrow access roads with hairpin turns, strict weight limits
- Helicopter transport for critical items and medical emergencies
- Limited laydown areas, just-in-time delivery required

## Mitigation Measures
- Pre-qualification and testing of equipment for altitude performance
- Seismic-resistant design with appropriate ductility factors
- Turbocharging and engine oversizing to compensate for derating
- Progressive acclimatization schedule for all workers
- Redundant logistics chains (road + air) for remote mountainous sites
