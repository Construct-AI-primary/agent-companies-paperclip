# Offshore & Marine Environments

## Environmental Conditions
- **Salt spray and exposure**: High chloride content accelerating corrosion of metals and concrete
- **Wave and tidal action**: Dynamic loading, tidal scheduling constraints, surge and swell
- **Submerged works**: Underwater foundations, marine growth, limited visibility
- **Weather windows**: Limited access windows, storm avoidance requirements

## Discipline-Specific Impacts
- **Civil/Structural**: Cathodic protection requirements, marine-grade concrete (low permeability, sulfate-resistant), tidal zone corrosion
- **Mechanical**: Marine propulsion systems, desalination, corrosion-resistant materials, hydraulic sealing
- **Electrical**: Subsea cables, salt-proof enclosures, lightning protection, corrosion to terminals
- **HSE**: Drowning risk, marine fauna, motion sickness, confined space on vessels

## Procurement Considerations
- **PO**: Marine-grade materials (duplex steel, sacrificial anodes), anti-fouling coatings, subsea equipment, vessel time
- **WO**: Diving services, marine logistics (PSVs, AHTS), corrosion inspection, marine mammal monitoring
- **SO**: Verify marine specifications (NORSOK, API), salt-spray test certificates, corrosion rates on confirmations

## Health & Safety
- Marine survival training (BOSIET/FOET), offshore medical certificates
- Overboard rescue procedures, man-overboard drills
- Diving safety, decompression illness awareness
- Sea survival suits for extreme conditions

## Logistics & Mobilization
- Vessel-based logistics with weather windows
- Port-to-site supply chains with contingency planning
- Helicopter crew transfer (limited cargo)
- Laydown areas limited by deck space

## Mitigation Measures
- Cathodic protection systems with regular potential surveys
- Marine-grade materials with corrosion allowances
- Tidal programming and weather window management
- Pre-installed subsea infrastructure during favorable seasons
- Redundant systems for critical marine operations
